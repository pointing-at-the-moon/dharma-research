## Abstract

This paper argues that the doctrine of "remote original attainment" (*jiǔyuǎn shíchéng* 久遠實成) disclosed in the *Lifespan Chapter* (如來壽量品) of the *Lotus Sūtra* must be read phenomenologically rather than as a personal-deity doctrine: the dharma-body (法身) is not an eternal being existing outside time, but the very ground on which temporal manifestation becomes possible. The paper proceeds along three lines. First, Zhiyi's 智顗 self-authenticating remark in the *Fahua xuanyi* 法華玄義—that the evidence for the ten subtleties of the original-gate (本門十妙) can be found "without far-seeking any other sūtra, within just a few pages" (就數紙之內), referring precisely to the Lifespan Chapter—is taken as structural bedrock: the Lifespan Chapter is not one section within the original-gate but is the original-gate in its entirety. Second, the simultaneous convergence of Vasubandhu's *Fahua lun* 法華論 T1520 phrase "the substance of the One Vehicle is the equal dharma-body" (一乘體即平等法身) with Kuiji's 窺基 *Fahua xuanzan* 法華玄贊 three-body classification completes, within the original-gate, the twin-track convergence thesis first formulated in S7-P08: the Tathāgatagarbha track and the Yogācāra track converge here for the first time in their full form. Third, Jizang's 吉藏 *Fahua yishu* 法華義疏 line "if one takes this sūtra as teaching that the Buddha is definitively permanent, that is itself the extreme view of eternalism" (若言此經明佛決定常者即是常見) is deployed as the primary defense against pedagogical hazard ❌ #5 (reading eternal lifespan as a Christian-style eternal God), with Dharmapāla's *Cheng weishi lun* 成唯識論 three-body passages as secondary support. A further methodological contribution is the refinement of the Āgama-leakage structure: the *Dīrgha-āgama*'s Seven-Buddha narrative, the human-lifespan gradient passage (80,000 → 20,000 → 100 years), and the *Ekottara-āgama* passage where the Buddha himself negates "because sentient beings' lifespans are short, the Tathāgata's lifespan is also short" together constitute a three-layer internal dialectic—framework-establishment, mechanism-provision, self-loosening—whose forward recuperation is precisely the original-gate disclosure of the Lifespan Chapter. The paper foreshadows the "from-star-to-black-hole" duality to be developed in S7-P12, and plants an undeveloped thread toward a future Pure Land project: "original lifespan without measure" (本壽無量) and Amitābha's "immeasurable life" (無量壽) are two faces of the same disclosure.

**Keywords:** Lifespan Chapter; remote original attainment; original lifespan without measure; ten subtleties of the original-gate; Zhiyi's "within just a few pages" self-authentication; phenomenological reading; dharma-body as ground of temporal manifestation; good-physician-sick-son parable (良醫病子喻); "pure land undestroyed while sentient beings see it burnt" (淨土不毀而眾見燒盡); Kuiji's three-body classification; Vasubandhu T1520 "one-vehicle substance equals equal dharma-body"; Jizang on the eternalist fallacy; Dharmapāla on the three bodies; Āgama leakage three-layer dialectic; twin-track convergence verified in the original-gate; relativistic time-frame (analogic only); pedagogical hazard #5; star-to-black-hole duality preview.

## I. Introduction

### 1.1 Statement of the Problem

From S7-P01 through S7-P10, ten consecutive Pointing-at-the-Moon papers have worked within the territory of the trace-gate (迹門). The opening of expedients in the *Skillful Means Chapter*, the burning house of the *Parables Chapter*, the destitute son of the *Faith and Understanding Chapter*, the universal rain of the *Medicinal Herbs Chapter*, the conjured city of the *Apparitional City Chapter*, the robe-jewel and topknot-jewel of the prediction series, the universal reverence of Sadāparibhūta, the responsive manifestations of the *Universal Gate Chapter*—the common structure of all ten is "how Śākyamuni speaks to sentient beings." Each chapter answers a pedagogical question: facing beings of different capacities, what speaking posture, what parable, what skillful arrangement does the Buddha adopt to lead them step by step toward the true mark of all dharmas (諸法實相)?

At the *Lifespan Chapter*, the gate changes.

This chapter no longer answers "how does Śākyamuni teach"; it answers "who is Śākyamuni." This is the only place in the entire *Lotus Sūtra* where the Buddha personally discloses his own temporal structure: "All worldly devas, humans, and asuras suppose that the present Śākyamuni Buddha left the Śākya palace, sat at the seat of awakening not far from Gayā, and attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. But, good sons, since I in fact attained Buddhahood, immeasurable, boundless hundreds of thousands of myriads of koṭis of nayutas of kalpas have passed" (T0262 fasc. 5).[^1] All beings—including every śrāvaka and bodhisattva present—suppose that the Śākyamuni before them today is the one who attained the Way under the bodhi tree forty years ago; in fact, since I actually became a Buddha, immeasurable, boundless kalpas have already passed.

Once this sentence is uttered, the center of gravity of the entire *Lotus Sūtra* shifts. Every skillful means, parable, and prediction of the first fifteen chapters is repositioned as a moment within the structure "a being who has already been a Buddha for immeasurable kalpas speaks to beings within time." Zhiyi accordingly divides the *Lotus Sūtra* into trace-gate and original-gate (本門): the first fourteen chapters are trace (the Buddha's manifestation), and the original-gate centered on the *Lifespan Chapter* is original (the Buddha's source). This paper takes up the inaugural disclosure of the original-gate.

### 1.2 Thesis Review and Position of This Paper

The Pointing-at-the-Moon S7 series advances three scholarly theses. This paper is the convergence point of Theses I and II within the original-gate.

**Thesis I (S7-P08 upgraded twin-track convergence):** The *Lotus Sūtra* is the first historical convergence of the Tathāgatagarbha track and the Yogācāra track in Mahāyāna Buddhism. The division of labor: the Tathāgatagarbha track carries the metaphysical ground of the equal dharma-body and original Buddhahood; the Yogācāra track carries the temporal mechanism of seed-perfuming and three-body manifestation. The shared pivot is dependent-arising nature (依他起性). In S7-P08's "simultaneous display of two extremes" treatment of the *Devadatta Chapter*, this convergence was first explicitly identified: the Dragon Girl's attainment (the quantitative wing) is carried by the Yogācāra track; Devadatta's prediction (the qualitative wing) is carried by the Tathāgatagarbha track.

This paper is the **first complete verification of that convergence within the original-gate**. The "original lifespan" of "original lifespan without measure" is the dharma-body face from the Tathāgatagarbha track; the three-body manifestation in time of the original lifespan without measure is the mechanism face from the Yogācāra track—both tracks converge in the *Lifespan Chapter* on the same object: the dharma-body is not a being within time, but the ground on which temporal manifestation occurs. S7-P08 established the convergence within a single chapter of the trace-gate; this paper extends it to the entire structure of the original-gate.

**Thesis II (Shè–Xiǎn duality):** The *Lotus* and *Avataṃsaka* form a star/black-hole radiation–collapse duality—*Lotus* radiates outward, speaking in forms beings can receive; *Avataṃsaka* collapses inward, the dharma-realm speaking its own language to itself. This paper is **the threshold of the transition from shè to xiǎn**. The temporal structure disclosed in the *Lifespan Chapter* already touches the edge of *Avataṃsaka*'s "one thought is the three times," yet the chapter still preserves the *Lotus*'s distinctive narrative posture of a father speaking to his children—the good-physician-sick-son parable being the proof. S7-P12 will formally develop the star-to-black-hole duality; this paper only paves the way.

**Thesis III (dramatic completion of the Āgama seed):** This paper makes a methodological refinement here. S1–S6 cast the Āgama–Mahāyāna relation as a temporal "seed → unfolding" structure; S8, turning to *Avataṃsaka*, recasts it as a "leakage" channel-strength structure (the same broadcast at differing signal strengths on different channels). This paper sits between the two: on the object of the *Lifespan Chapter*, the *Dīrgha-āgama* and relevant *Ekottara-āgama* passages can be read either as seed (temporal sequence) or as leakage (channel strength). More importantly, the three Āgama passages themselves constitute a "framework-establishment → mechanism-provision → self-loosening" internal dialectic—this is this paper's original refinement of Thesis III, detailed in §2.3.

### 1.3 Pedagogical Hazard Statement

This paper's principal front is ❌ #5: **remote original attainment misread as a Christian-style eternal God**.

The specific form of this misreading: taking "since I in fact attained Buddhahood, immeasurable, boundless hundreds of thousands of myriads of koṭis of nayutas of kalpas have passed" as "the Buddha is an eternal being who has existed beginninglessly," and then equating the Buddha with the monotheistic deity who "exists outside time, creates time, and is untouched by time." The danger is not merely doctrinal error—it would retroactively contaminate the teaching of the first fifteen chapters: if the Buddha is an eternal being, then skillful means, parables, and predictions all become "the eternal one's condescension to the finite," and the entire compassion structure of the *Lotus* would be replaced by the distance-feeling of monotheism.

This paper does not relegate the hazard to a footnote, nor handle it by indirection. Jizang in the *Fahua yishu* fourteen centuries ago already issued the direct rebuke: "If one takes this sūtra as teaching that the Buddha is definitively permanent, that is itself the extreme view of eternalism" (fasc. 10). §V will deploy this line as the primary force, with Dharmapāla's *Cheng weishi lun* three-body passages as auxiliary support, in a double defense that dismantles ❌ #5 head-on.

### 1.4 Structure Preview

§II presents canonical evidence in three layers: (a) three-translation collation of the *Lotus* base text; (b) four commentarial passages (Vasubandhu, Zhiyi's *Xuanyi*, Zhiyi's *Wenju*, Kuiji); (c) the three-layer Āgama-leakage dialectic. §III develops the core argument from Zhiyi's "within just a few pages" self-authentication, then unfolds the ten subtleties' specific correspondences in the *Lifespan Chapter*, the phenomenological reading of remote original attainment in three sub-arguments, and the complete convergence of Thesis I's twin-track structure within the original-gate. §IV runs one cross-disciplinary line: relativistic time-frames and the temporal manifestation of the dharma-body, strictly marked as heuristic analogy. §V handles ❌ #5 with double defense and exceptionally installs a failure-point marker (the first retroactive application of an S8 clause to S7). §VI closes the paper, previews the star–black-hole duality of S7-P12, and plants a Pure Land thread for future development.

## II. Canonical Evidence

The canonical basis is presented in three layers: (a) collation of three translations of the *Lotus* base text; (b) four commentarial passages from Vasubandhu, Zhiyi, and Kuiji; (c) three Āgama passages forming an internal dialectic. The order strictly follows the sūtra-before-śāstra principle. The Āgama material sits in layer (c) because this paper reads it as a leakage structure rather than as independent scriptural attestation; its function is to provide a ground-level counterpoint to the commentarial argument of layer (b).

### 2.1 Layer A — Three-Translation Collation of the *Lotus* Base Text

#### 2.1.1 The Three Passages of Remote Original Attainment in Kumārajīva's Translation

The core of the *Lifespan Chapter* (chapter 16) is three consecutively unfolding passages. The first overturns the temporal presupposition of the first fifteen chapters: "All worldly devas, humans, and asuras suppose that the present Śākyamuni Buddha left the Śākya palace, sat at the seat of awakening not far from Gayā, and attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. But, good sons, since I in fact attained Buddhahood, immeasurable, boundless hundreds of thousands of myriads of koṭis of nayutas of kalpas have passed" (T0262 fasc. 5). The Buddha first restates the entire temporal presupposition of his audience—Śākya palace, Gayā, seat of awakening, anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, four concrete spatiotemporal coordinates given in full—and then with three syllables ("but, good sons") overturns the entire coordinate system. The rhetorical posture is not "supplementary clarification" but "structural replacement": the teachings of the first fifteen chapters are not erroneous, but their temporal coordinate system must be re-encompassed by a larger one.

The second passage gives the spatial structure following the temporal overturning: "I have always been on Vulture Peak and other dwelling places" (常在靈鷲山及餘諸住處).[^2] Śākyamuni's parinirvāṇa is a manifestation on the channel of sentient beings, not an event at the level of the original lifespan; in the temporal structure of the original lifespan, the Buddha has never left Vulture Peak. The third passage pushes this to the extreme: "My pure land is undestroyed, while sentient beings see it burnt to ashes" (我淨土不毀，而眾見燒盡).[^3] The same spacetime obtains simultaneously on two channels—on the channel of sentient beings, this world is consumed by fire; on the Buddha's channel, the pure land remains intact. These are not two parallel worlds, but two manifestations of the same world under two postures of seeing. §3.3 will reconnect this passage to the seeing-essence (見精) structure of the *Śūraṃgama* developed in S6.

#### 2.1.2 The Good-Physician-Sick-Son Parable — A Skillful-Means Structure Internal to the Chapter

Immediately after the three passages of remote original attainment, the Buddha spontaneously gives the good-physician-sick-son parable (fasc. 5).[^4] This is structurally singular within the *Lotus*: every parable in the first fifteen chapters works at the position of "using a parable to teach sentient beings," whereas this parable works at the meta-level of "using a parable to explain what the Buddha has just said." The good physician, after administering medicine to his sons, journeys far away and falsely reports his own death, thereby shocking his deranged sons into taking the medicine and recovering—the object of the parable is the preceding "skillful manifestation of nirvāṇa": the Buddha's display of parinirvāṇa is not real extinction, but a responsive shock to the deranged condition of sentient beings.

It is especially worth noting that this parable is given by the Buddha himself, not produced by Mahākāśyapa or Śāriputra. At the very moment of the original-gate's first disclosure, the Buddha immediately supplies an accompanying skillful-means explanation—this is itself the Pointing-at-the-Moon style of self-authenticating structure: at the very instant the original-gate discloses itself, it also reminds the reader not to reify what is disclosed into another doctrine of "definitive permanence." The Buddha also, in a rhetorical self-gloss, establishes the doctrinal line that "skillful means ≠ falsehood": "Could anyone rightly accuse this good physician of the crime of falsehood?"[^5]

#### 2.1.3 The Twelve Grades of Merit in the *Distinguishing Merit Chapter*

The *Distinguishing Merit Chapter* (chapter 17) inherits the disclosure of the *Lifespan Chapter* and lists twelve grades of merit. The most crucial line: "such a one shall see the Buddha always present on Vulture Peak" (則為見佛常在耆闍崛山).[^6] For the reader who has profoundly believed and understood the *Lifespan Chapter*, the immediate seeing of the Buddha always on Vulture Peak follows directly—this transposes the "always on Vulture Peak" of §2.1.1 from a statement at the level of the Buddha into an immediate seeing at the level of the reader. The disclosure of the original-gate is not letting the reader know "so this is what the Buddha is," but letting the reader enter, here and now, the coordinate system that was always already there. The inverted-pyramid comparison in the same chapter—"a single thought of faith-understanding" outweighs "eighty myriads of koṭis of nayutas of kalpas practicing the five pāramitās"—hints that faith-understanding of the lifespan is itself prajñā made present[^7].

#### 2.1.4 Collation with the *Zhengfahua* and *Tianpin* Versions

Dharmarakṣa's T0263 *Rulai xianshou pin* (如來現壽品) renders the three core passages with "nashugai kalpa" (那術姟劫), "perfectly awakened" (至真等正覺), and "a man practicing the medical art" (士夫而為醫術) corresponding to Kumārajīva's "nayuta kalpa," "anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi," and "good physician"; the three core passages all align cleanly.[^8] Between the Western Jin old translation and the Later Qin new translation there is no structural divergence on the doctrinal content. The only difference between T0264 (the *Tianpin* version) and Kumārajīva's text is chapter numbering (15 vs. 16)[^9]. The collation conclusion: **remote original attainment is not an embellishment of Kumārajīva's personal translation style**; the core doctrine was already established at the Indic source level, and the phenomenological reading developed below does not depend on the rhetoric of any single translation.

### 2.2 Layer B — Four Indo-Chinese Commentarial Passages

#### 2.2.1 Vasubandhu's *Fahua lun* — The Eighth of the Ten Unsurpassed Meanings: "Unsurpassed Attainment of Great Bodhi"

Vasubandhu's *Saddharmapuṇḍarīka-upadeśa* T1519/T1520 places its systematic exegesis of the *Lifespan Chapter* not under the often-cited "seven kinds of merit" section, but under the eighth of the "ten unsurpassed meanings": "unsurpassed attainment of great bodhi" (成大菩提無上). The core proposition: "synonymy: the dharma-body is equal." The bodhi-attainment of the buddhas of the ten directions is wholly equal at the level of the dharma-body; all differences exist only in the temporal manifestation of the enjoyment-body (報身) and transformation-body (化身)[^10].

T1520 contains a further distinctive formulation: "The substance of the One Vehicle is the equal dharma-body" (一乘體即平等法身).[^11] This is the Vasubandhu-level attestation of Thesis I's twin-track convergence—the One Vehicle (the core of Thesis II's receptive face) and the equal dharma-body (the core of Thesis I's Tathāgatagarbha track) are already, at the Indic source level, bound as two faces of the same object. Vasubandhu further maps the three key lines of the *Lifespan Chapter* to the three Buddha-bodhis: the "I in fact attained Buddhahood" line corresponds to the reward-body bodhi; the "Tathāgata sees the marks of the three realms as they are" line corresponds to the dharma-body bodhi (i.e., the pure nirvāṇa of the Tathāgatagarbha nature)[^12]. §3.4 will unfold this line as the canonical bedrock of twin-track convergence.

#### 2.2.2 Zhiyi's *Fahua xuanyi* — The Ten Subtleties of the Original-Gate and the "Within Just a Few Pages" Self-Authentication

Zhiyi's *Fahua xuanyi* judges the *Lotus* as a symmetrical structure of ten subtleties of the trace-gate and ten subtleties of the original-gate[^13], the latter corresponding one-to-one with the former: original-cause subtlety, original-fruit subtlety, original-land subtlety, original-resonance subtlety, original-supernatural-power subtlety, original-Dharma-preaching subtlety, original-retinue subtlety, original-nirvāṇa subtlety, original-lifespan subtlety, original-benefit subtlety[^14]. The crucial discovery lies in Zhiyi's self-authentication: the citations for the ten subtleties of the original-gate "need not be far-sought from other sūtras… within just a few pages"—that is, within the single *Lifespan Chapter*[^15]. The structural import: all evidence for the ten subtleties lies within the few pages of the *Lifespan Chapter*; the chapter is not one section of the original-gate, the chapter **is** the original-gate in its entirety. §3.1 takes this line as structural bedrock.

Zhiyi further offers two key correspondence formulae: "opening the trace, disclosing the origin ↔ original-fruit subtlety" and "abiding in the trace, employing the origin ↔ original-lifespan subtlety,"[^16] and explains the trace-origin relation through two similes—the moon appearing in water, and pointing past a shadow to the sky[^17]. The moon is originally in the sky; its image appears in the water; the moon in the water is not another moon, but the same moon manifested relative to the water surface. This simile is directly isomorphic to the phenomenological reading of §3.3.

#### 2.2.3 Zhiyi's *Fahua wenju* — Sentence-by-Sentence Commentary

The *Fahua wenju* fasc. 5 contains dedicated commentary on "since I in fact attained Buddhahood" (pt5 L1939–1946)[^18] and "always on Vulture Peak" (pt5 L2493–2500)[^19]. The *Wenju*'s work is to ground the *Xuanyi*'s judgmental architecture in the textual surface; §3.2, in unfolding the ten-subtleties correspondences, will draw on relevant *Wenju* segments for granular support. The *Wenju*'s passage on the good-physician parable is also hit, providing commentarial backing for the meta-level skillful-means analysis of §2.1.2.

#### 2.2.4 Kuiji's *Fahua xuanzan* — Three-Body Classification

Kuiji's *Fahua xuanzan* fasc. 9 lower handles the *Lifespan Chapter* as a whole in lines 404–746, the core being three-body classification: the original Buddha is matched with the substance of the dharma-body; the trace Buddha with the function of the enjoyment- and transformation-bodies[^20]. The five-line exegesis of the dharma-body (lines 684–709) gives a five-fold delimitation—neither arising nor ceasing, neither one nor different, neither permanent nor annihilated—directly severing the misreading channel "dharma-body = eternal being within time"[^21]. Kuiji's work is the complete unfolding of Thesis I's Yogācāra track within the original-gate, and together with Vasubandhu T1520 forms a cross-shaped convergence: India–China, Tathāgatagarbha track–Yogācāra track.

### 2.3 Layer C — The Three-Layer Internal Dialectic of Āgama Leakage

This paper's handling of the relation between the Āgamas and the *Lifespan Chapter* constitutes a methodological refinement within the S7 series. S1–S6 treated the Āgama–Mahāyāna relation as a temporal "seed → unfolding" structure; S8, turning to *Avataṃsaka*, recast it as a leakage structure—differing signal strengths of the same broadcast on different channels. This paper sits between the two: the Āgama material relevant to the *Lifespan Chapter* can be read either as seed (temporal sequence) or as leakage (channel strength). More importantly, the three Āgama passages themselves constitute a **three-layer internal dialectic of "framework-establishment → mechanism-provision → self-loosening,"** whose forward recuperation is precisely the original-gate disclosure of the *Lifespan Chapter*.

#### 2.3.1 Layer One — The Seven Buddhas of the *Mahāpadāna*: Establishing the Kalpa Time-Frame

The *Mahāpadāna-suttanta* of the *Dīrgha-āgama* (T0001 fasc. 1) details the era, lineage, lifespan, and disciple count of the seven buddhas from Vipaśyin onward[^22]. The Āgama is here already employing kalpa-time as the temporal frame for the appearance of buddhas—the Buddha is not an object of historical time, but an object on the kalpa scale. This layer supplies the temporal-framework presupposition for the *Lifespan Chapter*'s "since I attained Buddhahood, immeasurable, boundless hundreds of thousands of myriads of koṭis of nayutas of kalpas": kalpa-scale temporal structure was already established in the Āgamas; the *Lifespan Chapter* is not abruptly introducing a new concept, but pushing the scale to its limit within an existing frame.

#### 2.3.2 Layer Two — The Lifespan Gradient: Coupling of Buddha-Lifespan and Sentient-Beings'-Lifespan

The same fascicle contains another crucial passage: in the era of Vipaśyin, human lifespan was 80,000 years; in Śikhin's, 70,000; … down to Śākyamuni's era, 100 years. What this discloses is not merely a temporal gradient but a **structural coupling mechanism**: the lifespan with which a Buddha appears is coupled to the lifespan of sentient beings of his era—when beings' lifespans are short, the Buddha's manifested lifespan is also short. The Āgama is here already speaking of "Buddha-lifespan is not a property of the Buddha himself but a co-constructed manifestation of Buddha and sentient beings"—although the Āgama does not state this explicitly, the structure is already operating in the text.

This layer refines the first: once the framework is established, the Āgama supplies its internal operating mechanism. The *Lifespan Chapter*'s "but since I in fact attained Buddhahood, immeasurable… kalpas have passed" is in fact a structural clarification of this coupling—the manifested lifespan is determined by coupling, while the original lifespan lies outside coupling's jurisdiction.

#### 2.3.3 Layer Three — The *Ekottara-āgama*: "Because Sentient Beings' Lifespans Are Short, the Tathāgata's Is Also Short," Negated by the Buddha Himself

The *Ekottara-āgama* fasc. 18 (cross-referenced with S8-P07) records a crucial dialogue: someone asserts that "because sentient beings' lifespans are short, the Tathāgata's lifespan is also short," and the Buddha counter-asks, "Why does he not abide for a kalpa?"—directly negating the proposition, and listing the "four inconceivables" as a warning[^23]. This layer is the Āgama's self-loosening of its own second layer: having established the coupling mechanism, the Āgama also concedes that the coupling is not absolute—the "door of requesting" opens precisely here. S8-P07 handles this passage from the *Avataṃsaka* angle of Samantabhadra's vow "requesting the Buddha to remain in the world," reading it as the negative image of the door of requesting. This paper rereads the same passage from the *Lotus*'s *Lifespan Chapter* angle: the seam the Āgama itself leaves here is the seam behind which the *Lifespan Chapter* gives the structure in positive form.

#### 2.3.4 Structural Import of the Three-Layer Dialectic

The three layers together form a complete internal dialectic: layer one **establishes the framework** (kalpa-time), layer two **supplies the mechanism** (coupling of Buddha- and sentient-beings'-lifespan), layer three **loosens itself** (the Buddha's self-negation of the absoluteness of coupling). The Āgama has, internally, already laid out all the presuppositions required for the original-gate disclosure—only the Āgama's channel did not unfold this structure in positive form; the *Lifespan Chapter* turns the same structure out from the seam in the Āgama and states it positively.

This three-layer dialectic supplies a structural reinforcement for Thesis III: the Āgama–Mahāyāna relation is not a one-way "seed germinating," nor purely a difference of channel strength, but rather **an internal dialectic within the Āgamas that finds its forward recuperation in Mahāyāna**. This refined version is reserved for the series-wrapping work of S7-P12.

## III. Core Argument

### 3.1 Zhiyi's Self-Authenticating Sentence: "Within Just a Few Pages"

The structural foundation of this paper rests on a single line from Zhiyi's *Fahua Wenju*: the original-gate has only *one* sūtra and only *one* object — the *Lifespan Chapter* itself, "within just a few pages." This is not Zhiyi's retrospective generalization; it is his self-authenticating declaration of the original-gate's scope.

This thesis has an immediate methodological consequence: research on the original-gate cannot proceed by "listing several original-gate sūtras and inducing common themes," because the original-gate has only one sūtra and only one theme. The work must concentrate entirely on structural analysis internal to the *Lifespan Chapter*. From §3.2 onward this paper proceeds by exactly this methodology.

### 3.2 The Concrete Unfolding of the Ten Original-Gate Marvels in the *Lifespan Chapter*

Zhiyi's ten original-gate marvels structurally parallel the ten trace-gate marvels. Two correspondence-pairs are decisive — and these are not Zhiyi's after-the-fact inductions but rules of trace–original conversion he himself explicitly marks: **"opening the trace to disclose the original ↔ marvel of original fruition"** and **"abiding-in-trace using original ↔ marvel of original lifespan."** This paper concentrates on these two.

**First pair: opening the trace to disclose the original ↔ marvel of original fruition.** The trace-gate's "disclosing the original" is the event of Śākyamuni attaining perfect awakening under the bodhi tree — the awakening seen by sentient beings, recorded by history, narrated by sūtras and treatises. The original-gate's "original fruition" is *that same* awakening at the level of original-lifespan-without-measure — not a *second* awakening event but the same event displayed on its other face. The key word is "open": the trace-gate's disclosure is not false, but it is a structure that has to be *opened* — once opened, a more fundamental "original fruition" is found inside. This opening is precisely the work performed by the *Lifespan Chapter*'s line "But, good sons, since I in fact attained Buddhahood…": it first affirms the awakening event sentient beings have seen, and then opens it from within.

**Second pair: abiding-in-trace using original ↔ marvel of original lifespan.** "Abiding-in-trace" is Śākyamuni's eighty-year manifest lifespan in the human world; "using original" is the use these eighty years make of original-lifespan-without-measure — the eighty years are not a *constriction* of the original lifespan but a single *use* of the original lifespan under specific conditions. This pair directly answers the internal Āgama dialectic handled in §2.3: the Āgama's second-layer coupling mechanism (Buddha-lifespan coupled to sentient-beings'-lifespan) speaks to the "abiding-in-trace" face; the Āgama's third-layer self-loosening (the Buddha's negation of the absoluteness of coupling) speaks to the "using original" face. A millennium before, Zhiyi had already articulated, in trace–original terms, the structure the Āgama left without positive disclosure.

The remaining eight marvels (original cause, original land, original responsiveness, original supernormal powers, original teaching, original retinue, original nirvāṇa, original benefit) structurally parallel the same form: each is "a face of the trace-gate ↔ that same face displayed at the level of original lifespan." Zhiyi's similes — the moon appearing in water, pointing past the shadow to the sky — are figurative treatments of precisely this "the same object simultaneously displayed at two levels": the moon is in the sky (original); the moon-in-water is not a second moon (trace) but the same moon's display relative to the water's surface; the moon-in-water is shattered by ripples (sentient beings see extinction), while the moon in the sky is undisturbed (always abiding on Vulture Peak).

### 3.3 A Phenomenological Reading of "Awakening from Distant Antiquity" — A Three-Layer Argument

The deepest argument of "awakening from distant antiquity" comes in §III. This paper claims: the doctrine should be read **phenomenologically**, not as **personalistic theism**. The core difference between the two readings lies in how they understand the relation between *time* and *dharma-body*.

- **Personalistic-theistic reading**: the dharma-body is an eternal *being* existing outside time, upon which time exerts no action. This reading reifies the dharma-body into "a being above time," exactly isomorphic to the theistic object of monotheist tradition — "existing outside time, creating time, untouched by time."
- **Phenomenological reading**: the dharma-body is *not* a being outside time; the dharma-body is **the ground of time's appearing itself**. The relation between dharma-body and time is not "a being and its external attribute" but "the ground of appearing and appearing itself." Under this reading, "awakening from distant antiquity" does not say "the Buddha is a being who has been alive for measureless kalpas" but rather "the structure of the Buddha's appearing is unconstrained by any particular stretch of time; the appearing in any stretch of time is at once a cross-section of the appearing across measureless kalpas."

This section supports the phenomenological reading with three layers.

#### 3.3.1 Layer One — "Since" Names a Time-Structure, Not a Time-Origin

The "since" in "since I in fact attained Buddhahood, measureless, boundless hundreds of thousands of myriads of koṭis of nayutas of kalpas have passed" is easily misread as a *temporal starting point* — as if to say, "from some immeasurable-kalpas-ago point up to the present." But on close reading, no statement of an origin precedes "since," and what follows is not "until now, this long" but rather "measureless, boundless… kalpas." This time-quantity is not the result of measuring from a starting point to an endpoint; it is a description of the structure of time itself.

Compare the seven-Buddhas passage of the *Mahāpadāna-suttanta* (§2.3.1): the Āgama is already using a kalpa-time framework, and the "past" in "ninety-one kalpas in the past" of Vipaśyin Buddha is in the strict sense a temporal origin-statement. The *Lifespan Chapter*'s "since" is different — it gives no origin, only a boundless time-quantity. This is not a simple amplification of the Āgama's mode of expression; it is a different *type* of time-statement: the Āgama speaks of "an event that occurred at some past time," the *Lifespan Chapter* speaks of "the non-determinacy of time itself with respect to this object."

#### 3.3.2 Layer Two — "Always Abiding on Vulture Peak" and "Sentient Beings See It Burning Up" Hold Simultaneously — Linking S6-P07's Seeing-Essence Structure

"My pure land is not destroyed, yet sentient beings see it burnt up" is the most direct textual support for the phenomenological reading. The same space-time, under two viewing postures, simultaneously presents two completely opposite appearings — this structure can be held without contradiction only when "viewing posture" is taken into the description.

S6-P07 already handled the same problem from the seeing-essence (見精) structure of the *Śūraṃgama Sūtra*, fasc. 1: the phenomena seen by the eye-faculty (the assembly, the world, the Buddha as Ānanda saw them) and the seeing-essence itself are not objects of the same level. The seeing-essence is not a "subject that can see"; it is the structural condition for "appearing being able to *be* appearing." When the seeing-essence is occluded, sentient beings see only the gathering and dispersing at the phenomenal level; when the seeing-essence is recognized, the phenomenal-level gathering and dispersing can no longer shake the seeing-essence itself.

The *Lifespan Chapter*'s "the pure land is not destroyed, yet sentient beings see it burnt up" is directly isomorphic to the *Śūraṃgama* seeing-essence structure: the burning-up on the sentient-being channel corresponds to the phenomenal-level gathering and dispersing; the un-destroyed pure land on the Buddha channel corresponds to the unshaken seeing-essence level. The two are not parallel worlds; they are the same world simultaneously displayed at two structural levels. This paper reads the *Lifespan Chapter* as an independent articulation, internal to the *Lotus*, of the *Śūraṃgama*'s seeing-essence structure — the two sūtras enter the same structure from different language-systems.

This cross-reference has a methodological consequence: the phenomenological reading of "awakening from distant antiquity" is not an external import by 指月, but a structure already present within Mahāyāna sūtras; *Lotus* and *Śūraṃgama* are structurally isomorphic on this point.

#### 3.3.3 Layer Three — The Self-Authenticating Structure of the Good-Physician-and-Sick-Sons Parable

The location of the good-physician-and-sick-sons parable was already noted in §2.1.2: it is the meta-level skillful-means clarification the Buddha himself proactively gives. This section makes a further point: the very *existence* of this parable is the phenomenological reading's self-authentication.

If "awakening from distant antiquity" were a personalistic-theistic statement — if the Buddha really were an eternal being, and the manifestation of extinction his condescension to sentient beings — the parable would not need to appear. An eternal being need not explain why he appears to undergo birth and death; he can simply declare his essence. The parable appears precisely because "awakening from distant antiquity" is not an essence-statement but a **self-clarification of an appearing-structure**: in the same moment as the original-gate is opened, the Buddha proactively uses a parable to tell the reader not to reify what was just said into another doctrine of *determinate permanence*. The parable's phrase "feigning to say his body had died" is the boldest line in the entire *Lotus*: in the clearest possible language, the Buddha tells the reader that the manifestation of extinction is a structural pedagogical device, not a factual declaration that "the Buddha has not really died."

This self-authenticating structure corresponds exactly to 指月's own principle, "Those who can see, can see; those who cannot, cannot": the disclosure of the original-gate is not a fact that can be received as information, but a structure the reader must enter for themselves. This is also why the twelvefold gradation of merit in the *Distinguishing Merits Chapter* takes "they then see the Buddha always present on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa" as the apex of the deep-mind-faith stratum — deep-mind-faith is not "believing this doctrine"; it is entering, here and now, that coordinate system.

### 3.4 The Complete Closure of Thesis I's Twin-Track Confluence in the Original-Gate

S7-P08 *Both Ends Displayed Simultaneously*, in handling the *Devadatta Chapter*, first identified the twin-track confluence structure of Thesis I: the tathāgatagarbha track and the Yogācāra track first systematically converge in the *Lotus*. But P08's identification was completed within a single trace-gate chapter — the *Devadatta Chapter*, however pivotal, is in the end one segment of the skillful-means series. This paper extends the twin-track confluence to the *whole structure* of the original-gate: the two tracks converge not merely in a single trace-gate chapter but on the *whole object* of the original-gate.

**The closure-point of the tathāgatagarbha track in the original-gate** is the line in Vasubandhu T1520: "the body of the One Vehicle is precisely the equal dharma-body." This line binds the core of Thesis II (the One Vehicle) and the core of Thesis I's tathāgatagarbha track (the equal dharma-body) into two faces of the same object at the level of the Indian original. Reading the *Lifespan Chapter* back through this line: the "original lifespan" of original-lifespan-without-measure is not the lifespan of the Buddha-as-individual; it is the *time-dimension* of the equal dharma-body — the dharma-body's appearing in any time-slice is the complete entrance of the entire One Vehicle. Jizang's *Fahua Yishu* phrase "the body of the dharma-body which is the tathāgatagarbha" runs the same direction, only in the language of the Three-Treatise system.

**The closure-point of the Yogācāra track in the original-gate** is Kuiji's three-body adjudication in the *Fahua Xuanzan*: the original Buddha is paired with the *substance* of the dharma-body, the trace Buddha with the *function* of the enjoyment-body and transformation-body, and the three bodies are neither one nor different. Kuiji's work is not to abstract the dharma-body into a still-higher being, but to read the original-gate's "original" and the trace-gate's "trace" as two display-modes of the same dharma-body within different conditioning structures. The substance of three-body adjudication is **the description of an appearing-mechanism** — the dharma-body as the ground of appearing, the enjoyment- and transformation-bodies as the concrete forms of appearing; the two are not two objects but two faces of the same object under different viewing conditions. This mode of description is precisely where the Yogācāra track excels.

**The confluence-point of the two tracks** is *the same object*: the dharma-body is not a being *in* time but the *ground of time's appearing*. The tathāgatagarbha track approaches this object from "the body of the One Vehicle is the equal dharma-body"; the Yogācāra track approaches it from "the appearing-mechanism of the three bodies, neither one nor different." On the whole structure of the original-gate, the two paths converge at the same point. This convergence is not 指月's construction; it is a closure already completed by the joint work of Vasubandhu, Jizang, and Kuiji. 指月's work is only to identify this already-completed closure across the three masters' different language-systems and to name it positively.

The original-gate is therefore not merely the structural center of the *Lotus*. It is, in the entire history of Mahāyāna Buddhism, the first place where the two great tracks — tathāgatagarbha and Yogācāra — completely converge on a *single object*. This is the strongest verification, in the present paper, of the upgraded version of Thesis I.

## IV. Cross-Disciplinary Convergence

This paper takes only one cross-disciplinary line: the time coordinate-system of relativity and the time-appearing of the dharma-body. This line is strictly marked as a heuristic analogy, with no claim of theoretical equivalence.

### 4.1 Relativity's Time Coordinate-System — The Abandonment of Absolute Time

Einstein's special relativity discloses: there is no absolute time. Time is not a background existing independently of the observer; it is a coordinate-system *coupled* to the observer's state of motion. The temporal measurements of the same event by two observers in relative motion necessarily differ — not as measurement error, but as the structural result of time itself being a coordinate-system. General relativity further couples the structure of time with the curvature of spacetime: time flows more slowly in strong gravitational fields, not as a mechanical fault of clocks but as a change in the spacetime metric itself.

Relativity's deepest shock to everyday intuition lies in its requirement that we **abandon "absolute time" as the premise of an independent background**. Time is no longer a container "within which events occur"; time becomes "a dimension of the structure of events themselves." ⚠️ This abandonment is not a denial that time exists, but a denial that time has an absolute substantiality independent of the structure of observation.

### 4.2 Structural Analogy — Not Theoretical Equivalence

The phenomenological reading of the *Lifespan Chapter* structurally shares this same abandonment: the abandonment of "absolute Buddha-lifespan" as a property of the Buddha himself, independent of the capacities of sentient beings. Buddha-lifespan is not an objective property of the Buddha but an appearing co-constructed by Buddha and sentient beings; the "without-measure" of original-lifespan-without-measure is not a maximally large measurement-value but a structural statement of "time's non-determinacy with respect to the original lifespan."

⚠️ **Strict marking**: this is only a structural analogy, not a theoretical equivalence. Relativity is physics' mathematical description of the spacetime metric; the *Lifespan Chapter* is Buddhism's religious-phenomenological description of the dharma-body's appearing. The two are non-equivalent at every level:

- **Object**: relativity treats physical spacetime; the *Lifespan Chapter* treats the dharma-body's appearing.
- **Method**: relativity uses mathematical modeling; the *Lifespan Chapter* uses doctrinal analysis of sūtras and commentaries.
- **Purpose**: relativity aims to predict physical phenomena; the *Lifespan Chapter* aims to disclose the structure of liberation.
- **Verification**: relativity is verifiable by physical experiment; the *Lifespan Chapter*'s verification lies in the reader's own deep-mind-faith.

**Non-reducibility statement**: relativity cannot be used to "prove" awakening from distant antiquity, and the *Lifespan Chapter* cannot be used to "predict" relativity. 指月's work here is only to notice that the two share, at the level of abstract form, the structural feature of "abandoning the premise of absolute time" — that this structural feature *is shared* is itself worth noting, and nothing more.

### 4.3 A Deeper Common Direction

The two share another, deeper common direction: **the abandonment of the premise that "the observer is excluded from the object."** Newtonian mechanics assumes that the observer can stand outside the universe and measure time; relativity puts the observer back into spacetime — the observer's state of motion becomes part of time-measurement. Some readings of the Āgamas and early Mahāyāna read the Buddha as an "object that sentient beings can observe from outside"; the phenomenological reading of the *Lifespan Chapter* puts sentient beings' viewing-posture back into the Buddha's appearing-structure — sentient beings are not observers standing outside the Buddha; their viewing-posture is itself a constitutive part of the appearing of Buddha-lifespan.

This common direction extends the arguments of §3.3.2 (seeing-essence structure) and §3.3.3 (the self-authenticating structure of the good-physician parable) from inside Buddhism out to a wider range: the abandonment of "the observer external to the object" is a structural move shared by the twentieth-century revolution in physics and the original-gate disclosure of the Mahāyāna sūtras. ⚠️ Again to emphasize: sharing this move does not mean the two are saying the same thing. Physics' abandonment is at the level of mathematical structure; the Mahāyāna sūtras' abandonment is at the level of liberation-structure; there is no reductive relation between the two levels.

Linking back to a related insight from S8-P11: quantum non-locality shocks physicists because physicists have not yet abandoned the premise of "separation"; Buddhism never built that premise. The *Lifespan Chapter*'s abandonment of absolute Buddha-lifespan is isomorphic — not that Buddhism predicted physics, but that Buddhism was never *inside* the premise that needs to be abandoned.

## V. Middle-Way Correction

This section addresses ❌ #5 head-on: the misreading of *jiǔyuǎn shíchéng* (久遠實成, "originally awakened in the remote past") as an eternal Christian-style God. This paper does not relegate this hazard to a footnote, nor handle it through lateral evasion — Jizang already finished fighting this battle on the front line fourteen hundred years ago. The work of this section is simply to combine Jizang's battlefield with Dharmapāla's three-body structure into a doubled defence.

### 5.1 The Specific Form and Range of ❌ #5

Pedagogical hazard #5 takes this concrete form: reading "since I in fact attained Buddhahood, incalculable, boundless hundreds of thousands of myriads of *koṭis* of *nayutas* of *kalpas* have passed" as "the Buddha is an eternal being who has existed beginninglessly." This misreading has four typical symptoms:

1. Imagining the dharma-body as "a being outside time," thereby reifying the dharma-body
2. Reading the manifested *parinirvāṇa* as "an eternal being's condescension to finite beings," distorting the structure of compassion into a structure of distance
3. Reading all skillful means as "the great being's stooping to the lower beings," wrecking the *Lotus Sūtra*'s father–child narrative posture
4. Retroactively re-contaminating the entire teaching of the first fifteen chapters — if the Buddha is an eternal being, then skillful means, parables, and predictions all become unidirectional operations of an eternal being upon finite beings

This misreading is not a marginal deviation; it retroactively reads the entire *Lotus Sūtra* as a monotheistic structure. The hazard range covers the whole original-gate (本門) and must be dismantled head-on.

### 5.2 Main Defensive Line — Jizang's Mādhyamika Refutation

In *Fahua yishu* (法華義疏) fasc. 10, while handling the Lifespan chapter, Jizang leaves a sentence that directly calls out the error:

> If one says this sūtra teaches that the Buddha is determinately permanent, that is precisely the eternalist view (*śāśvata-dṛṣṭi*).[^24]

The structure of this sentence is a strict Mādhyamika refutation: if the sūtra is read as "the Buddha permanently abides as a determinate entity," that is not an understanding of the sūtra but a fall into eternalism — and eternalism is the heretical view (*tīrthika*), the heretical view is wrong view. Jizang leaves no evasive room here: the personalistic-theistic reading is not an acceptable interpretive direction for the *Lotus Sūtra*; it is directly judged as heretical eternalism. Earlier in the same fascicle Jizang has already judged the substance (*tǐ* 體) of the *Lifespan Chapter* as "the dharma-body which exceeds the four propositions and cuts off the hundred negations," and opens four propositions to release the three lifespans (dharma-body-lifespan, transformation-body-lifespan, reward-body-lifespan, and the final "neither-skillful-nor-real" that dissolves all three into quiescence)[^25] — the positive Three-Treatise construction of the three bodies and three lifespans.

Jizang's argumentative framework here is the Madhyamaka eight negations: not arising, not ceasing; not permanent, not annihilated; not one, not different; not coming, not going. The sūtra's "original-lifespan-without-measure" (本壽無量) is not "the original lifespan determinately abides permanently" — the opposite of "permanent" is not "impermanent" but "neither-permanent-nor-impermanent." The "without-measure" of original-lifespan negates precisely the very concept "determinately permanent," because "determinately permanent" already reifies the original lifespan into an object subject to permanent/impermanent binary judgment. The true original lifespan does not fall into this binary; thus to say it "permanently abides" is wrong, and to say it is "impermanent" is also wrong. The sūtra's positive language — "incalculable, boundless hundreds of thousands of myriads of *koṭis* of *nayutas* of *kalpas*" — is a third kind of statement that exceeds the permanent/impermanent binary.

Jizang further deploys the *catuṣkoṭi*-with-hundred-negations: one cannot say the original lifespan "is" some temporal quantity, cannot say it "is not" some temporal quantity, cannot say it "both is and is not" some temporal quantity, cannot say it "neither is nor is not" some temporal quantity. With all four corners refuted, what remains is not nothingness but the original lifespan itself, which cannot be captured by the four corners — the *tathāgatagarbha*-suchness body steps onto the front stage here.

This work of Jizang's is decisive for this paper's handling of ❌ #5: the core error of the personalistic-theistic reading is to read the original lifespan as "determinately permanent," and Jizang head-on judges this reading as eternalism. Pointing-at-the-Moon need only re-cite Jizang's verdict in this paper's context and mark its structural position. Jizang has already finished fighting the battle.

### 5.3 Auxiliary Defensive Line — Dharmapāla's Yogācāra Analysis of the Three-Body Structure

The dharma-body section of Dharmapāla's *Cheng weishi lun* (成唯識論) fasc. 10 (four passages: L348–357 / L363–379 / L388–401 / L419–449) constructs the three-body structure positively from a Yogācāra angle: self-nature body (*svabhāvika-kāya*, dharma-body), enjoyment body (*sāṃbhogika-kāya*, reward-body), and transformation body (*nairmāṇika-kāya*, manifestation-body). The point of the three-body structure is not "there are three Buddhas" but **to take the relation between dharma-body and temporal manifestation as a structural description.**

- **Self-nature body:** the truly pure dharma-realm of all *Tathāgatas*, the equal support of enjoyment and transformation, free from marks, quiescent, severed from all conceptual elaborations[^26]. The self-nature body does not arise and cease in time, but not because it "transcends time" — rather because it is the very ground of temporal manifestation. The ground does not lie within what it grounds. This is a structural statement, not a temporal one[^27].
- **Enjoyment body and transformation body:** the two modes in which the self-nature body manifests within causal-conditional structures. The enjoyment body is the sublime body manifested for the bodhisattva assembly; the transformation body is the responsive body manifested for all sentient beings. Both manifest in time, but both manifestations take the self-nature body as their ground[^28].
- **The three bodies are not-one-not-different:** not three Buddhas, nor one Buddha divided into three aspects; the three bodies are a triple naming of the same object under different manifestation structures.

Dharmapāla's work and Jizang's work form the division of labour of the doubled defence: **Jizang dismantles the personalistic-theistic reading (refutation, 破)**; **Dharmapāla positively establishes the three-body structure (establishment, 立)**. Without refutation, the personalistic-theistic reading flows back from the side; without establishment, the reader falls into the other trap of nihilism ("if the Buddha is not an eternal being, then what is the Buddha after all?"). Neither line can be omitted.

### 5.4 Incidental Treatment of ❌ #1 — Sectarian Superiority

The Lifespan chapter is often read as the highest evidence for "the *Lotus* is the king of all sūtras" — only the *Lotus* discloses the original awakening in the remote past, therefore the *Lotus* is the highest scripture. This paper's position: the temporal structure disclosed by the Lifespan chapter is equally present in different modes within Huayan's "one thought is the three times" and Śūraṃgama's seeing-essence (§3.3.2 has already given the structural-isomorphism argument). The *Lotus*'s uniqueness is not "only the *Lotus* has an original-gate" but "only the *Lotus* discloses the original-gate in the posture of a father speaking to his children." This is a functional difference, not a hierarchical one. Sectarian-superiority discourse is naturally treated here — this paper need not devote a separate section to refuting it; it is enough that the structural-isomorphism argument is in place.

## VI. Conclusion

### 6.1 Yields of This Paper

This paper completes work on four levels. **At the level of canonical basis,** the three-layer structure (A·sūtra / B·śāstra / C·Āgama seepage) is fully unfolded, especially layer C's three-layer internal Āgama dialectic (establishing the framework → giving the mechanism → self-loosening), which is a methodological refinement of Proposition Three; this refined version is reserved for the closing of the S7-P12 series. **At the level of core argument,** Zhiyi's "within just a few pages" self-attestation is established as the structural ground — the Lifespan chapter is not a chapter of the original-gate but the original-gate's whole unfolding; the original-gate has only one object, and the ten marvels of the original-gate are simply ten angles upon that single object. The phenomenological reading of *jiǔyuǎn shíchéng* is positively established through three layers of argument ("since" as temporal structure, isomorphism with the seeing-essence, and the good-physician parable as self-attestation). The closure of Proposition One's dual-track convergence within the original-gate receives its strongest verification at the cross-junction of Vasubandhu T1520's "the body of the One Vehicle is precisely the equal dharma-body" with Kuiji's three-body classification; the upgraded version of S7-P08 completes its extension here from a single chapter of the trace-gate to the original-gate as a whole. **At the level of interdisciplinary resonance,** the structural analogy between relativity's temporal coordinate systems and the manifestation structure of original-lifespan is strictly marked as heuristic; the two share two structural features — "abandonment of the absolute-time premise" and "abandonment of observer externality" — but no theoretical equivalence is asserted. **At the level of middle-way correction,** ❌ #5 is dismantled head-on — Jizang's "if one says this sūtra teaches that the Buddha is determinately permanent, that is precisely the eternalist view" is the main line, Dharmapāla's three-body structure is auxiliary; the doubled defence of "refutation" and "establishment" cannot omit either; ❌ #1 sectarian superiority is naturally treated through the structural-isomorphism argument.

### 6.2 S7 Series Progress

After S7-P11 is complete, the *Saṃgraha · Lotus Sūtra* series of S7 has only S7-P12 (the dissemination section) remaining. This paper is the inaugural opening of the original-gate; P12 will carry the dissemination section together with the closure of the entire S7 series, and will formally develop the bridging of Proposition Two (*saṃgraha*–manifestation duality) toward the S8 Huayan series.

### 6.3 Bridging S7-P12 and S8 — Preview of Star to Black Hole

The temporal structure disclosed by the Lifespan chapter has already touched the edge of Huayan's "one thought is the three times" and "interpenetration without exhaustion." The crucial difference lies not in doctrinal content but in **posture** —

- **Lotus (*saṃgraha*):** outward radiation. Even at the deepest place of the original-gate, *jiǔyuǎn shíchéng*, the Buddha still speaks in the narrative posture of "a father speaking to his children"; the appearance of the good-physician parable is the very evidence. The whole language of the *Lotus* is modulated under the requirement that "sentient beings be able to receive it." This is the posture of a star: emitting the inner immeasurable information outward in the form of electromagnetic waves that sentient beings can receive.
- **Huayan (manifestation):** inward collapse. The dharma-realm speaks its own language and no longer adjusts its channel to suit the receiver. The "reading difficulty" of the *Avataṃsaka* is not a textual problem but a structural one — the *Avataṃsaka* is not speaking to sentient beings; the *Avataṃsaka* is the dharma-realm unfolding upon itself within its own structure. This is the posture of a black hole: the information density has already exceeded the upper limit any external channel can carry.

The Lifespan chapter is the chapter within the *Lotus* nearest to Huayan, yet it still preserves the father's posture — the "I" of "since I in fact attained Buddhahood" and the call "good sons" are both narrative. This narrative posture is precisely the dividing line between *Lotus* and Huayan: beyond this line, the narrative posture itself is replaced by the self-unfolding of the *dharmadhātu*. S7-P12 will formally develop the star–black-hole duality; this paper here only paves the way.

### 6.4 The Pure Land Hidden Thread — 本壽無量 ↔ 無量壽

In the Abstract and §1.4 this paper plants a hidden Pure Land thread: original-lifespan-without-measure (本壽無量) and Amitābha's "lifespan-without-measure" (無量壽, *Amitāyus*) come from a single source. This paper does not unfold this thread, for three reasons. First, the core task of this paper is the original-lifespan structure of the original-gate's inaugural opening; unfolding the Pure Land direction would scatter the argumentative focus. Second, "lifespan-without-measure" has its own independent doctrinal structure within the transmission of the Pure Land school, requiring an independent paper (or even an independent series) to handle. Third, Pointing-at-the-Moon's future Pure Land project has not yet formally launched; this paper only plants the seed without watering it. This hidden thread is reserved for formal development when the future Pure Land project launches.

### 6.5 A Single Closing Sentence

Original-lifespan-without-measure is not a factual statement about the Buddha; it is a structure into which the reader must enter on his own. All the arguments of this paper work outside the structure; the work inside the structure is left to the reader.
