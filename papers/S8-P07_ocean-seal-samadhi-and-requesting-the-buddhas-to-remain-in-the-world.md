**The Seventh Vow as a Direct Acknowledgment of the Sealing-Function**

## Abstract

This paper argues that the seventh of Samantabhadra's Ten Great Vows—"Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World" (*qǐng fó zhù shì* 請佛住世)—is not a petition for an external Buddha to extend his physical lifespan, but a direct acknowledgment of the structural fact that the sealing-function (*néng yìn zhī yòng* 能印之用) of Ocean-Seal Samādhi (*hǎiyìn sānmèi* 海印三昧) manifests moment-to-moment without interruption. The argument proceeds in three layers.

First, returning to the original sūtra sequence in the "Bodhisattva Xianshou" chapter (*Xiánshǒu pǐn* 賢首品) of the *Avataṃsaka Sūtra*, the paper shows that the triad *Ocean-Seal → Huayan Samādhi → Indra's Net* is already fully articulated within the scripture itself, requiring no reliance on later commentarial reconstruction. This is the original argument advanced in §II.A.1.

Second, taking the eight characters "*pútí pǔ yìn zhū xīnxíng, shì gù shuō míng wéi zhèngjué*" 菩提普印諸心行，是故說名為正覺 ("Bodhi universally seals all mental activities; therefore it is called perfect awakening") from the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter (*Rúlái chūxiàn pǐn* 如來出現品) as its doctrinal anchor, the paper demonstrates that within Huayan doctrine perfect awakening is not an achieved state but an ongoing sealing-function. These same eight characters constitute the scriptural origin-point of the *Gate of Subtle Inclusion and Establishment*.

Third, juxtaposing the Āgama's Cāpāla-shrine episode—where Ānanda's three silences permanently forfeit the Buddha's offer to remain "a kalpa or more"—with Samantabhadra's seventh vow, the paper shows that the Āgama's "gate of requesting" is a one-time, time-bounded event threshold, whereas Huayan's "gate of requesting" is elevated, through the *three-time radiant manifestation* (*sānshì bǐngxiàn* 三世炳現)—the sixth of Chengguan's ten meanings in the *Huayan jing shu*—into a structural gate present in every moment. This reversal does not negate the Āgama: the rain-drenched night at Cāpāla did occur, and the Buddha's *parinirvāṇa* at Kuśinagara is an unalterable historical fact. Huayan simply replaces these facts within the temporal structure of the Ocean-Seal, where "the past Buddha entering *nirvāṇa*" and "the present Buddha remaining in the world" are sealed simultaneously rather than sequentially.

Drawing on Fazang's *Huayan wujiao zhang* 華嚴五教章 (fasc. 4)—"this rests upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi, whence all is brilliantly and simultaneously manifested"—the paper further argues that *Ocean-Seal is not one gate among the Ten Profound Gates; it is the foundation on which all ten gates are established*. Fazang's sixteen-character definition in the *Xiū Huáyán àozhǐ wàng jìn huányuán guān* 修華嚴奧旨妄盡還源觀—"true thusness is the inherent awakening; when delusion is exhausted and the mind is clarified, all images manifest simultaneously"—together with the *Huayan jing tanxuan ji*'s judgment that "the Gate of Perfectly Bright Ocean-Seal Samādhi is the root from which great functioning is creatively instituted," confirms Ocean-Seal's status as the creative foundation of Huayan doctrinal functioning.

§VI then translates this doctrinal position into three practical scenarios—the first thought upon waking, the act of prostration, and encountering a hostile person—to demonstrate how the seventh vow is enacted in daily practice through the simultaneous operation of *subtlety–inclusion–establishment*: **acknowledgment, not petition, is the vow's concrete action**. This paper continues the construction of Ocean-Seal as Huayan's scriptural body (*néng quán jiào tǐ* 能詮教體) begun in S8-P01, and provides the three-layered doctrinal articulation—sealing-function, structural face, systemic face—on which S8-P08 (*Six Characteristics in Perfect Fusion*) and S8-P11 (*The Ten Great Vows under the Ten Profound Gates*) will build.

**Keywords:** Ocean-Seal Samādhi (*hǎiyìn sānmèi*), Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World, Samantabhadra's Ten Great Vows, Manifestation of the Tathāgata Chapter, Bodhisattva Xianshou Chapter, bodhi universally sealing all mental activities, three-time radiant manifestation, Gate of Subtle Inclusion and Establishment, Chengguan's tenfold exegesis of the Ocean-Seal, Ānanda's three silences at Cāpāla, one's own mind possessing Buddhas attaining enlightenment moment by moment, sealing-as-function, Fazang, Chengguan, *Wàng jìn huányuán guān*, Gate of Perfectly Bright Ocean-Seal Samādhi, acknowledgment not petition

## I. Prologue: The Four-Line Verse of the Xianshou Chapter

The "Bodhisattva Xianshou" chapter of the *Avataṃsaka Sūtra*, after a long cascade of "if … then …" ascending verses, turns to a summary passage on how a bodhisattva, having reached accomplishment, "manifests responsively according to kind" (*suílèi shìxiàn* 隨類示現). Bodhisattva Xianshou, answering Mañjuśrī's question on the merit of the stage of faith, speaks of how bodhisattvas—through the power of their great compassionate vows—enter all *samādhis*, universally display spiritual transformations, and liberate sentient beings. He concludes the entire passage on responsive manifestation with a single four-line verse:

> The bodily forms of sentient beings each differ,
> Their karmic deeds and vocal sounds likewise are boundless;
> That all of these can be fully manifested—
> This is the awesome spiritual power of Ocean-Seal Samādhi.[^1]

This is the *first* passage in the entire eighty-fascicle *Avataṃsaka Sūtra* in which the term "Ocean-Seal Samādhi" appears as the thematic crown of a passage. What the verse articulates is not a particular bodhisattva's particular samādhic experience, but a structural proposition: **given that the forms of sentient beings differ infinitely and their karma and speech are immeasurable, how can a bodhisattva, within a single thought-moment, manifest universally across the ten-direction buddha-lands? Because there is something—its name is Ocean-Seal Samādhi—which is not an "ability" but *manifestation itself*.**

But what matters more than the verse itself is what comes *immediately after*.

Having stated the thematic verse on Ocean-Seal Samādhi, Bodhisattva Xianshou continues without pause:

> Adorning inconceivable buddha-lands,
> Making offerings to all the Tathāgatas,
> Emitting great light without bound,
> Liberating sentient beings without limit.
> … All this is attained in sovereign freedom
> Through the power of the Buddha's Huayan Samādhi:
> Within a single mote of dust entering *samādhi*,
> Perfecting the *samādhis* of all motes of dust,
> And that mote of dust does not increase,
> Yet within the one, inconceivable lands are universally displayed.[^2]

From "the awesome spiritual power of Ocean-Seal Samādhi" to "through the power of the Buddha's Huayan Samādhi," there is no transition whatsoever in the original text—these two verse-segments are flush against each other. And then, immediately after the Huayan Samādhi verses, comes:

> Within that single dust-mote, many lands:
> Some with buddhas, some without buddhas,
> Some defiled, some pure, …
> Some like the heat-shimmer of a wilderness,
> Some like the Indra-Net of the heavens.[^3]

From Ocean-Seal, to Huayan, to Indra's Net—**this logical chain is already fully present within the original sūtra, requiring no reliance on later commentarial construction whatsoever**.

This is the first original argument of S8-P07: **the three-samādhi system "Ocean-Seal → Huayan → Ten Profound Gates" has its scriptural origin-point right here in this continuous verse-passage of the Xianshou chapter**. Fazang's later declaration in the *Huayan wujiao zhang*—"this rests upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi, whence all is brilliantly and simultaneously manifested"[^4]—and Chengguan's tenfold exegesis of the Ocean-Seal in the *Huayan jing shu*[^5] are doctrinal developments of this passage; all of them can be traced back to the "manifestation–subtlety–inclusion" sequence already latent in the Xianshou chapter.

Why then is the pairing of this paper not "Ocean-Seal Samādhi × Gate of Simultaneous Mutual Correspondence" but rather "Ocean-Seal Samādhi × Vow Seven: Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World"?

Because the true test of Ocean-Seal Samādhi lies not in the affirmative proposition "it manifests all things," but in its sharpest negative counter-case: **the Buddha's entry into *parinirvāṇa***. If Ocean-Seal Samādhi truly is the sealing-function of the *dharmadhātu* manifesting thought-moment by thought-moment, then why did Śākyamuni Buddha enter *parinirvāṇa* between the twin *śāla* trees at Kuśinagara? Why, when the *Mahāparinirvāṇa-sūtra* (the Āgama version) records that the Buddha gave the sign three times and Ānanda remained silent three times, did the Buddha sternly rebuke Ānanda with the words "the fault is precisely yours"? Why, once the Buddha had announced "I shall enter final *nirvāṇa* three months hence," could this no longer be reversed?

The seventh of Samantabhadra's Ten Great Vows—"Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World"—is Samantabhadra's answer, delivered on the Huayan assembly, to precisely this question. **It is not a request that some external buddha extend his physical lifespan; it is a direct acknowledgment of the structural fact that the sealing-function manifests without cease moment by moment.** Vow Seven is the positive reversal of the Āgama's "requesting the Buddha to remain" passage: the Āgama's gate-of-requesting was once a missed opportunity (after Ānanda's three silences, no later request could avail); the Huayan's gate-of-requesting is still there *in every single thought-moment*.

What this paper undertakes is to unfold this reversal—from Āgama to Huayan, from petition to acknowledgment, from external buddha to the Ocean-Seal of one's own mind—into a doctrinal structure that can enter daily practice. The paired Profound Gate is *Wēixì xiāngróng ānlì mén*, because the pivot of this reversal lies in two characters from the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter: **universal sealing** (*pǔ yìn* 普印).

## II. Scriptural Basis

### A. Sūtra Sources — The Huayan Scripture Itself

#### II.A.1 The Ocean-Seal Verse of the Xianshou Chapter

The complete context of the Xianshou chapter's Ocean-Seal verse has been quoted in §I. What must be made explicit here is that this verse-passage belongs to fascicle 14 of the eighty-fascicle *Avataṃsaka* (*Xianshou pin* 12.1), the concluding segment of Bodhisattva Xianshou's reply to Mañjuśrī concerning the merit of the stage of faith.[^6] Doctrinally, this passage is the paradigmatic "accomplishment of practice" (*xíng chéngjiù* 行成就) following upon "accomplishment of faith" (*xìn chéngjiù* 信成就)—the answer to the question: after faith is complete, how does the bodhisattva manifest responsively across the ten-direction buddha-lands? The answer is Ocean-Seal Samādhi.

This location is decisive: **within the doctrinal architecture of the Huayan school, Ocean-Seal Samādhi is never "a state only those in high stages can speak of"—in the original sūtra it is placed squarely within the faith-stage section of the Xianshou chapter**. This point will be fully developed in S8-P05 (*The Subsumption of Faith-Completion*, via Fazang's *Huayan wujiao zhang* argument that "the buddha of practice is not the buddha of stage," *xíng fó fēi wèi fó* 行佛非位佛). In P07, it suffices to note: Ocean-Seal is not the exclusive property of a few sagely bodhisattvas; it is a samādhi that a bodhisattva at the stage of faith has *already* entered—and this fact is itself the scriptural ground of the fifth Middle-Way correction in §V.

#### II.A.2 The Definitional Passage in the Manifestation of the Tathāgata Chapter

Fascicle 52 of the *Avataṃsaka*, "Manifestation of the Tathāgata, Part Three,"[^7] is the *only* passage in the entire eighty-fascicle scripture where "Ocean-Seal" is used to define "perfect awakening" (*zhèngjué* 正覺) directly. Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, re-illuminating the meaning of the Tathāgata's perfect awakening, declares:

> Just as the great ocean universally seal-manifests the bodily forms of all sentient beings across the four continents, and is therefore called "the great ocean"—so too is the bodhi of all buddhas: it universally manifests all sentient beings' mental activities, faculties, and desires, yet has nothing manifested. Therefore it is called the bodhi of all buddhas. …
>
> Bodhi is one mark, eternally markless,
> Yet within it, immeasurable bodies arise;
> As the ocean seal-manifests sentient beings' bodies,
> So bodhi universally seals all mental activities—
> And therefore it is called perfect awakening.[^8]

Within this passage, there are eight characters which are the core anchor of the entire S8-P07 paper:

> **菩提普印諸心行，是故說名為正覺。**
> *Pútí pǔ yìn zhū xīnxíng, shì gù shuō míng wéi zhèngjué.*
> *Bodhi universally seals all mental activities; therefore it is called perfect awakening.*

The doctrinal weight of these eight characters lies in this: they define "perfect awakening" *directly as* the action of "universally sealing all mental activities." **Perfect awakening is not a state that has been attained; it is an ongoing sealing-function that is now being exercised.** The two characters *pǔ yìn* "universal sealing" are the scriptural origin-point of Fazang's later naming of the Profound Gate *wēixì xiāngróng ānlì mén*: *pǔ* "universal" corresponds to *xiāngróng* "mutual inclusion"; *yìn* "sealing" corresponds to *ānlì* "establishment"; and *wēixì* "subtlety" is the precision with which this universal sealing-function acts upon each sentient being's each mental activity.

Further, the prose section of the same fascicle states:

> Buddha-son, the bodhisattva-*mahāsattva* should know: within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening. Why is this? Because the buddhas, the Tathāgatas, do not depart from this mind in order to attain perfect awakening. As with one's own mind, so too the minds of all sentient beings: all possess the Tathāgata attaining equal perfect awakening, vast and all-pervading, present in every place, neither departing nor severed, without rest, entering the inconceivable gate of skillful means.[^9]

"*Within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening*" (*zì xīn niàn niàn cháng yǒu fó chéng zhèngjué* 自心念念常有佛成正覺)—this line has been cited repeatedly across the generations by Fazang, Chengguan, and Zongmi. It is one of the most direct scriptural witnesses of the Huayan doctrine of *nature-arising* (*xìng qǐ* 性起), and it is the principal scriptural text of S8-P04. But in P07 it carries another weight: **it is the doctrinal foundation of Vow Seven**. If one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, always has a buddha attaining perfect awakening, then "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World" cannot possibly refer to some external buddha—it must be an acknowledgment, right here and now, of this sealing-function that one's own mind is already exercising thought-moment by thought-moment.

#### II.A.3 The Dynamic Face in the Manifestation of the Tathāgata Chapter

Ocean-Seal is not a static mirror-image. The same passage in the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter provides three further verse-clusters supplementing the dynamic structure of the sealing-function:

> Just as worlds undergo formation and destruction,
> Yet empty space neither increases nor decreases;
> All buddhas emerge into the world,
> And the mark of bodhi is likewise.[^10]

—formation and dissolution flow past while empty space is undiminished; all buddhas emerge into the world and enter *nirvāṇa* while the mark of bodhi neither increases nor decreases. This is the *invariant face* of the sealing-function.

> As with the sentient beings of the three times' kalpas and lands,
> All their mental activities and faculty-desires—
> Bodies equal in number to all these are manifested;
> Therefore perfect awakening is called immeasurable.[^11]

—the infinite mental activities and faculties of all sentient beings across the three times are all met, in Ocean-Seal, with a corresponding body. This is the *inexhaustible face* of the sealing-function.

Fascicle 51 contains a further important notice: among the "nine thunder-sounds" preceding the Buddha's exposition of dharma is a sound named "the thunder-sound of Ocean-Seal Samādhi."[^12] This explicitly locates Ocean-Seal Samādhi as the *dynamic preparation* before the Buddha's teaching, not as its product or residue. Ocean-Seal is the sealing-function itself, not a manifested image; it is the ground of teaching, not the fruit of teaching.

### B. Treatise Sources — Indian Origins

The doctrine of Ocean-Seal Samādhi is extraordinarily thin in the Indian treatise corpus. Neither the Yogācāra treatises of Vasubandhu and Asaṅga nor the Madhyamaka treatises contain any extended discussion under the name "Ocean-Seal." **This doctrinal thinness is itself evidence of the Huayan school's creative institution**: the doctrinal system of Ocean-Seal Samādhi was constructed by Chinese patriarchs working directly from within the *Avataṃsaka Sūtra* itself, not transplanted from Indian treatises. This contrasts instructively with S8-P08 (*Six Characteristics in Perfect Fusion*), whose Indian origin in Vasubandhu's *Daśabhūmika-vyākhyāna* (*Shídì jīng lùn* 十地經論) is explicit; for Ocean-Seal, no such Indian antecedent exists.

The doctrinal significance of this fact is the following: **the Fazang–Chengguan lineage of Ocean-Seal doctrine is one of the methodological manifestos of the Huayan school as an independent Chinese Buddhist doctrinal-classification school**—it demonstrates that the Chinese patriarchs were capable of constructing an independent doctrinal system directly from scripture, without mediation by an Indian treatise.

### C. Treatise Sources — Chinese Patriarchs

#### II.C.1 The Sixteen-Character Definition in Fazang's Wàng jìn huányuán guān

In the *Xiū Huáyán àozhǐ wàng jìn huányuán guān* 修華嚴奧旨妄盡還源觀, Fazang provides what is at once the most concise and the most complete Huayan definition of Ocean-Seal in sixteen characters:

> As for "Ocean-Seal": it is **true thusness, inherent awakening. When delusion is exhausted and the mind is clarified, all images manifest simultaneously.** It is like the great ocean: when wind stirs waves, nothing is clearly reflected; when the wind subsides, the ocean-water is pellucid, and no image fails to appear.[^13]

Three key terms in these sixteen characters warrant special marking:

First, **"true thusness, inherent awakening"** (*zhēnrú běnjué* 真如本覺): Ocean-Seal is not some ability that must be cultivated into being; Ocean-Seal is the inherently-awake face of true thusness itself. "Inherent awakening" means it has never been manufactured—it can only be recognized.

Second, **"when delusion is exhausted and the mind is clarified"** (*wàng jìn xīn chéng* 妄盡心澄): on the surface this may seem to state "the goal of practice is to empty out deluded thoughts," but placed within the overall Huayan doctrinal architecture, this is *not* describing a linear sequence of "first delusion, then its exhaustion, then the appearance of Ocean-Seal." Rather: **the moment the very oppositional relation between "delusion" and "awakening" is itself recognized as constructed is the moment of "delusion's exhaustion."** The grounds for this reading lie in another of Fazang's works, the *Huayan jin shizi zhang* 華嚴金師子章: the relation between "delusion" and "awakening" is not a sequential relation but a simultaneous relational relation.

Third, **"all images manifest simultaneously"** (*wànxiàng qí xiàn* 萬象齊現): the character *qí* "simultaneously, in one stroke" is the key. Not "manifesting in sequence," but "manifesting *together*." That all images are presented in one and the same present moment—this *simultaneity* is the internal structural identity between Ocean-Seal Samādhi and the first of the Ten Profound Gates, the *Gate of Simultaneous Mutual Correspondence* (*tóngshí jùzú xiāngyīng mén* 同時具足相應門).

#### II.C.2 The "Ocean-Seal Radiant Manifestation Gate" and the "Gate of Perfectly Bright Ocean-Seal Samādhi" in the Tanxuan ji

In the *Huayan jing tanxuan ji* 華嚴經探玄記, Fazang places Ocean-Seal Samādhi at two of the most decisive locations in the entire Huayan doctrinal architecture.[^14]

The first location is in fascicle 1, within the "tenfold classification of the teaching-body" (*jiào tǐ shí mén* 教體十門). Fazang classifies the scriptural body (*néng quán jiào tǐ*) of the entire *Avataṃsaka* into ten gates, of which the *ninth* is the **"Gate of Ocean-Seal Radiant Manifestation"** (*hǎiyìn bǐngxiàn mén* 海印炳現門). In other words, what ultimately allows the *Avataṃsaka* to function *as* scripture—its very scriptural body—rests finally on this gate. This elevates Ocean-Seal from "a passage within a single chapter" to "the teaching-body of the entire Huayan scripture."

The second location is the "Gate of Perfectly Bright Ocean-Seal Samādhi" (*yuánmíng hǎiyìn sānmèi mén* 圓明海印三昧門) in fascicle 4, which contains a decisive sentence:

> The Gate of Perfectly Bright Ocean-Seal Samādhi: **the root from which great functioning is creatively instituted** (*chuàng biàn dà yòng zhī běn* 創辨大用之本).[^15]

These five characters—*chuàng biàn dà yòng zhī běn*—mark Ocean-Seal's true position within Huayan doctrine: **it is the root on which "great functioning" (*dà yòng*) can be instituted, distinguished, and unfolded**. Without Ocean-Seal, there is no great functioning of the Ten Profound Gates; without Ocean-Seal, there is no unfolding of the Six Characteristics in Perfect Fusion; without Ocean-Seal, the entire doctrinal architecture of the Huayan school has no place to stand.

In the same passage Fazang employs a striking simile—the *asura's fourfold army*: when the asuras battle Indra, they can manifest four distinct battle-formations simultaneously in empty space, omitting nothing, while empty space itself is neither augmented nor diminished. Fazang writes: the functioning of Ocean-Seal Samādhi is like this asura's fourfold army—all images manifest simultaneously within Ocean-Seal, yet Ocean-Seal itself (like empty space) neither increases nor decreases.[^16] This simile is more precise than the common "mirror reflecting objects" trope, because it emphasizes both *simultaneity* and *non-augmentation-non-diminution*.

#### II.C.3 The General Meaning of the Profound Gates in the Huayan wujiao zhang

In the "Ten Profound Gates" section of fascicle 4 of the *Huayan wujiao zhang*, Fazang delivers a judgment that has been endlessly cited in subsequent generations:

> Now these ten gates are one and the same: dependent origination, unobstructed, perfectly fused. Wherever one gate is present, all are contained. … The first, the Gate of Simultaneous Mutual Correspondence, … **rests upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi, whence all is brilliantly and simultaneously manifested**.[^17]

The key of this sentence lies not in its explanation of the Gate of Simultaneous Mutual Correspondence, but in the placement of the four characters *cǐ yī hǎiyìn sānmèi* "this rests upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi." What Fazang is really stating here is a claim that governs the entire Huayan doctrinal architecture: **the first of the Ten Profound Gates, "Simultaneous Mutual Correspondence," is able to stand only because it rests upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi; and because the first gate is the master-gate of the Ten, with the remaining nine being its unfoldings, all ten gates ultimately rest upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi.**

This is the "Ocean-Seal as general meaning of the Ten Profound Gates" doctrine that §IV will unfold: **Ocean-Seal is not one gate among the Ten Profound Gates (as some later readings have mistakenly taken it); Ocean-Seal is the root on which the Ten Profound Gates become possible**. Chengguan, in the *Huayan jing shu*, systematizes this general doctrine further, developing it into the "tenfold exegesis of Ocean-Seal"—which is precisely the subject of §III.

## III. Main Framework — Chengguan's Tenfold Exegesis of the Ocean-Seal (Selected Meanings)

In fascicle 16 of the *Dàfāngguǎng fó huáyán jīng shū* 大方廣佛華嚴經疏, Chengguan, building on Fazang's doctrinal-classification foundation, unfolds Ocean-Seal Samādhi into *ten meanings* (*shí yì* 十義) and systematizes them. The ten meanings are not ten parallel sub-items but ten facets of observation through which the single sealing-function operates simultaneously—and this compositional form is itself a demonstration of "subtle inclusion and establishment": in letter the meanings are ten; in doctrine they are one.

This section does not unpack each meaning in sequence. It selects four that serve as the doctrinal pivots for §IV, §V, and §VI. The remaining six are noted in the footnotes without further exposition.

### III.1 First Meaning — Depth of Essence, Like the Ocean

Chengguan names the first meaning "depth of essence, like the ocean" (*tǐ shēn rú hǎi* 體深如海).[^18] The character *shēn* "depth" is the pivot—it does not describe some "very deep" samādhic experience; it states: **the essence of Ocean-Seal is so deep that no measuring mind can reach its floor**.

This "bottomlessness" corresponds to the phrase "bodhi is one mark, eternally markless" from the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter. It is not that no marks appear, but that the very *category* of "mark" loses its grip at this depth. Chengguan develops further: the ocean, though deep, manifests all marks; precisely because it is deep, it can contain; precisely because it is deep, the marks it manifests are not stained by the ocean itself. This structure of "manifesting without being stained" is the first intrinsic feature of Ocean-Seal as sealing-function.

*Bridge to S3 Yogācāra readers:* Yogācāra describes the *ālayavijñāna* as "ever-turning like a torrent" (*héng zhuǎn rú pùliú* 恒轉如瀑流)—a torrent is the *horizontal* continuity of time. Huayan describes Ocean-Seal as "depth of essence, like the ocean"—depth is the *vertical* bottomlessness of the subject-object relation. The two are not the same simile, but they point to the same thing: **that root which manifests all things without being limited by any of them**. This point is developed further in §IV.1.

### III.2 Third Meaning — All Images Manifest Simultaneously

The third meaning, "all images manifest simultaneously" (*wànxiàng qí xiàn* 萬象齊現), directly echoes the four characters *wànxiàng qí xiàn* in Fazang's *Wàng jìn huányuán guān*.[^19] Chengguan stresses here: *qí* "together, in one stroke" does *not* mean "simultaneously arranged in a row," but "simultaneously without mutual obstruction." **The reason all images can manifest together is that each single image is itself an entry-gate to the whole of the *dharmadhātu*.**

This reading elevates "manifesting together" from a visual description to a doctrinal proposition: every "image" manifest within Ocean-Seal is *not* "a part of Ocean-Seal" but "the whole of Ocean-Seal presenting itself at this one position." Therefore "all images manifesting together" really means "the whole manifesting together within each image"—which is the earliest prototype, within the Ocean-Seal doctrine, of the later formula "one is all, all is one" (*yī jí yīqiè, yīqiè jí yī* 一即一切，一切即一).

This meaning becomes the doctrinal pillar of the practical unfolding of the Gate of Subtle Inclusion and Establishment in §VI.

### III.3 Sixth Meaning — Three-Time Radiant Manifestation

The sixth meaning, "three-time radiant manifestation" (*sānshì bǐngxiàn* 三世炳現), concerns how the sealing-function handles the structure of time.[^20] Ocean-Seal does not manifest only "the present"; Ocean-Seal manifests past, present, and future *simultaneously*—but the word "simultaneously" here carries a special sense: **not that the three times are crammed into a single present, but that present, past, and future each retain their own positions while all being sealed at once**.

Chengguan cites as witness the line from the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter, "within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening"—the two characters *niàn niàn* "thought-moment after thought-moment" are the smallest unit of three-time radiant manifestation. Each thought is a single radiant-manifestation point at which "past buddhas have already attained perfect awakening," "present buddhas are now attaining perfect awakening," and "future buddhas will attain perfect awakening" are all sealed together.

**This meaning is the doctrinal foundation for the Āgama-to-Huayan reversal in §IV.3:** the reason Āgama's "requesting the Buddha to remain" can be reversed in Huayan into a request *exercisable right now* is that the temporal structure of Ocean-Seal Samādhi is originally three-time radiant manifestation—"the past Buddha's entry into *nirvāṇa*" and "the present Buddha's remaining in the world" are not, within Ocean-Seal, two sequential events but one simultaneous structure.

### III.4 Tenth Meaning — Ultimately Inexhaustible

The tenth meaning, "ultimately inexhaustible" (*jiūjìng wújìn* 究竟無盡), is the closing of the ten meanings.[^21] "Inexhaustible" does not mean "quantitatively limitless"; it means *structurally*: "there is no position whatsoever from which one can say 'Ocean-Seal ends here.'" The sealing-function has no external limit, because anything that could be set as "external" is already being sealed *within* Ocean-Seal.

This meaning is the doctrinal preparation for the later "dharmadhātu of unhindered phenomena-to-phenomena" (*shì shì wú ài fǎjiè* 事事無礙法界), but within P07 its more direct significance is this: **it proves that Vow Seven cannot possibly be limited by the fact "the Buddha has already entered *nirvāṇa*"**—because the very fact "the Buddha has entered *nirvāṇa*" is itself being sealed, continuously, within the ultimate inexhaustibility of Ocean-Seal.

### III.5 The Remaining Six Meanings

The second meaning ("markless yet manifesting"), fourth ("mutual reflection of retribution and environment"), fifth ("subject and object interpenetrating"), seventh ("co-arising without obstruction"), eighth ("sovereign transformational functioning"), and ninth ("host-guest perfect fusion")—these six form the middle spine of the tenfold exegesis and are not unfolded here.[^22] It should be noted that within Chengguan's system, each of these six meanings corresponds to the local operational mechanism of one or several of the Ten Profound Gates; their complete unfolding belongs properly to S8-P11 (*The Ten Great Vows under the Ten Profound Gates*), where the entire structure of the Ten Gates is treated together. This paper does not duplicate that treatment.

## IV. Doctrinal Interlocking

### IV.1 Ocean-Seal — Huayan — Ten Profound Gates as a General Doctrine

The sentence in fascicle 4 of Fazang's *Huayan wujiao zhang*—"this rests upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi, whence all is brilliantly and simultaneously manifested"[^23]—is the decisive original text for establishing "all Ten Profound Gates rest upon Ocean-Seal." But the full weight of this sentence can be seen only when it is placed back into the original sūtra-sequence of the Xianshou chapter.

The verse-sequence of the Xianshou chapter runs: *Ocean-Seal* (the thematic statement of manifestation) → *Huayan Samādhi* ("entering samādhi within a single mote of dust"—the subtle unfolding of manifestation) → *Indra's Net* ("within that single dust-mote, many lands"—the mutual inclusion and mutual entry of manifestation). **There is no transition in the original sūtra between these three—the three segments are flush against each other.** Fazang's judgment in the *Wujiao zhang* is, essentially, the doctrinal formalization of this sūtra-sequence: Ocean-Seal is the *capacity*; Huayan is the *subtle unfolding of the capacity's manifestation*; the Ten Profound Gates are the *full deployment of inclusion in the capacity's manifestation*. The reason the first gate, "Simultaneous Mutual Correspondence," is the master-gate of the Ten is precisely that it takes its *simultaneity* directly from Ocean-Seal; and since the first gate is itself the general rubric of the remaining nine, the remaining nine also take their simultaneity *indirectly* from Ocean-Seal—this is the doctrinal chain establishing "all Ten Profound Gates rest upon Ocean-Seal."

In other words: **Ocean-Seal is not one gate within the Ten Profound Gates; Ocean-Seal is the root of all ten**. The Gate of Subtle Inclusion and Establishment paired with this paper is no exception—what it takes from Ocean-Seal is the *precision-facet* of the sealing-function as it acts upon each sentient being's each mental activity, that is, the facet signified by *pǔ yìn* "universal sealing."

### IV.2 The Dynamic Face of Ocean-Seal — Not a Static Mirror-Image

"As a mirror reflects objects" is a common simile used in later exegesis of Ocean-Seal, but this simile has a structural weakness: a mirror is *static*, *passive*, waiting for the objects that come before it. If Ocean-Seal were only this, then "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World" would degrade into "asking the mirror to keep being there"—a reading at once impotent and mistaken.

The three verse-clusters of the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter make the dynamic face of Ocean-Seal quite clear:

*First,* "just as worlds undergo formation and destruction, yet empty space neither increases nor decreases"—Ocean-Seal is not a fixed mirror-surface; Ocean-Seal is *that non-increasing-non-decreasing dimension within the flux of formation and destruction*. It is not stillness; it is "motion that neither increases nor decreases."

*Second,* "as with the sentient beings of the three times' kalpas and lands, all their mental activities and faculty-desires, bodies equal in number to all these are manifested"—Ocean-Seal does not wait for sentient beings to come and be reflected; Ocean-Seal *actively manifests bodies*. Every mental activity of every sentient being is responded to, in that very moment, by a corresponding body-mark. "Manifest" here is a verb, not a noun.

*Third,* the "nine thunder-sounds" passage in fascicle 51 lists "the thunder-sound of Ocean-Seal Samādhi" as one of the dynamic preparations *preceding* the Buddha's exposition of dharma.[^24] Thunder is not passive; thunder is the very kinetic force that shakes empty space into motion. Ocean-Seal, as the kinetic force preceding exposition, is the *dharmadhātu*'s own awakening-motion *before* teaching—not a mirror-image left behind *after* teaching.

Putting these three together: **Ocean-Seal is the dynamic sealing-function, actively manifesting, actively responding, actively shaking the *dharmadhātu* into the condition of exposition, at every single present moment**. This dynamic face is precisely the doctrinal ground on which Vow Seven can stand—because what you are requesting is *not* a still mirror, but this shaking, manifesting, responding sealing-function itself.

### IV.3 The Āgama's Negative Image and the Huayan's Positive Reversal

This is the central pivot-section of P07 and the passage where the paper's doctrinal interlocking is tightest.

#### IV.3.1 The Āgama Negative — The Rain-Drenched Night at Cāpāla

The *Dīrgha-āgama*'s *Mahāparinirvāṇa-sūtra* (*Yóuxíng jīng* 遊行經) records[^25] that, at the Cāpāla shrine (*Cāpāla-caitya*) near Vaiśālī, the Buddha addressed Ānanda three times:

> Ānanda, whoever has cultivated the four bases of miraculous power, practiced them diligently, borne them in mind without forgetting, and set the will toward them—such a one may remain "a kalpa or more." Ānanda, the Tathāgata has cultivated the four bases of miraculous power to the utmost, borne them in mind, set the will upon them: the Tathāgata is able to remain a kalpa or more, dispelling darkness for the world, bringing manifold benefit, giving peace to gods and humans.

The Buddha said this three times. **Ānanda was silent three times.** After the third utterance, receiving no response, the World-Honored One sternly said to Ānanda:

> Ānanda, go. Know that it is now the time.

After Ānanda departed, Māra Pāpīyas came to request the Buddha to enter *parinirvāṇa*, and the Buddha consented. When Ānanda returned and again requested the Buddha to remain, the Buddha said:

> What has already been cast off, already discharged—there is no place for the Tathāgata to contradict his own word. It is like a householder who has spat food upon the ground: will he take it up and eat it again? No. … Ānanda, *the fault is precisely yours*.

"*The fault is precisely yours*" (*zhèng yóu rǔ gù* 正由汝故)—the responsibility is laid on Ānanda. In the parallel passage on Śāriputra's prior *parinirvāṇa* in fascicle 18 of the *Ekottara-āgama*, a structurally similar arrangement reappears:[^26] the Buddha asks rhetorically, "Why do I not remain for a kalpa?" and rejects the explanation "because sentient beings' lifespans are short, the Tathāgata's lifespan is also short."

What these two Āgama passages jointly display is the doctrinal structure: **"requesting to remain" does indeed have a "gate of requesting"—but this gate is time-bounded; once missed, it cannot be recovered**. Ānanda's three silences are precisely that miss. This is "requesting to remain" at the Āgama level—an opportunity, an opportunity that once existed but has been forfeited.

#### IV.3.2 The Huayan Positive Reversal

On the Huayan assembly, the seventh of Samantabhadra's Ten Great Vows—"Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World"—occupies a doctrinal position utterly different. It is not catching up on an opportunity already missed; it is acknowledging a fact that has never been missed and cannot possibly be missed: **within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening**.

Juxtaposing Āgama and Huayan:

| Facet | Āgama · Cāpāla | Huayan · Samantabhadra's Vow Seven |
|---|---|---|
| The gate of requesting | One-time, time-bounded | Present in every thought-moment |
| The object requested | Śākyamuni's physical lifespan | The *dharmadhātu* Ocean-Seal as sealing-function |
| Meaning of "remaining in the world" | Extension of bodily presence for "a kalpa or more" | Acknowledging the moment-by-moment buddha attaining perfect awakening |
| Failure mode | Ānanda's three silences → permanent forfeit | (Structurally) unlossable, but may be obscured |
| Responsibility structure | "The fault is precisely yours" (external indictment) | Direct acknowledgment within this very thought (internal seeing) |

The Āgama's negative image is reversed, in Huayan, into a positive image. **The pivot of this reversal is not that Huayan negates the Āgama—Huayan does not deny the Āgama's historical facts. The rain-drenched night at Cāpāla did occur; Ānanda was indeed silent three times; Śākyamuni Buddha did enter *parinirvāṇa* at Kuśinagara**. The reversal takes place at the doctrinal level: the Āgama's "gate of requesting" is a structural *event-threshold*; Huayan elevates this event-threshold into a structural *time-structure*—**the gate is there all along; every thought-moment is the gate**.

The doctrinal ground of this reversal lies in §III.3, the sixth meaning: "three-time radiant manifestation." Because the temporal structure of Ocean-Seal is three-time radiant manifestation, "the past Buddha's entry into *nirvāṇa*" and "the present Buddha's remaining in the world" are, within Ocean-Seal, not sequential but simultaneous. The Buddha's entry into *nirvāṇa* is itself being sealed, continuously, within Ocean-Seal—it does not vanish, but neither can it obstruct the "buddha attaining perfect awakening" of this present thought-moment from being sealed simultaneously alongside it.

In other words, Vow Seven is *not petition* but *acknowledgment*. **The true action of the four characters "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World" is turning the mind toward the sealing-function that is already operating, not requesting some external thing to change its state.**

### IV.4 Implicit Linkage to the Twenty-Five Perfect Penetrations of the *Śūraṅgama*

This subsection is only a marginal note and is not developed in detail. The S6 paper-series has already completed its analysis of the Twenty-Five Perfect Penetrations (*ershíwǔ yuántōng* 二十五圓通) of the *Śūraṅgama Sūtra*. The position of Ocean-Seal Samādhi within the Huayan system corresponds structurally to that of Avalokiteśvara's *Perfect Penetration of the Ear-Faculty* (*ěrgēn yuántōng* 耳根圓通) among the twenty-five: both take the *subject-capacity*—the one who manifests, the one who hears—itself as the core, rather than taking what is manifested or what is heard as the core. Readers who have read the S6 treatment of ear-faculty perfect penetration may fruitfully read that series' "turning the hearing to hear one's own nature" (*fǎnwén wén zìxìng* 反聞聞自性) alongside the present chapter's "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World = direct acknowledgment of the sealing-function." At doctrinal depth, the two series converge.[^27]

## V. Middle-Way Corrections

### ❌ V.1 Ocean-Seal ≠ a Passive Mirror-Image

The most common misreading is to imagine Ocean-Seal as a still mirror, waiting for the ten thousand images to come and be reflected. On the surface this reading is "intuitive"; in fact it misses Ocean-Seal's core facet as *sealing-function*.

As §IV.2 has shown, the three verse-clusters of the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter together with the "nine thunder-sounds" passage of fascicle 51 state explicitly: Ocean-Seal is a *dynamic* manifestation-function, the kinetic preparation before the exposition of dharma, the actively manifesting and actively responding sealing-function itself. A mirror is passive, still, undifferentiated; Ocean-Seal is active, vibrant, responsive to each sentient being's each mental activity. Reading Ocean-Seal as a mirror leads directly to the impotent and erroneous conclusion "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World = asking the mirror to stay there."

Correct reading: Ocean-Seal is the *dharmadhātu*'s own moment-by-moment awakening-motion; the mirror simile is the lowest rung of skillful means and cannot be the stopping-point.

### ❌ V.2 Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World ≠ Extending Physical Lifespan

The second danger lies in the two characters *zhù shì* "remaining in the world." If the reader takes "remaining in the world" to mean "the physical body continuing to exist in the world," then Vow Seven immediately degrades into a petition concerning lifespan—and such a petition is powerless before the historical fact: Śākyamuni Buddha's body already entered *parinirvāṇa* at Kuśinagara; this fact is unalterable.

The "remaining in the world" of Samantabhadra's Vow Seven is not in the sense of physical presence; it is in the sense of the *dharmadhātu*—it is the acknowledgment of the structural fact "within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening." The prose section of the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter states, "*the buddhas, the Tathāgatas, do not depart from this mind in order to attain perfect awakening*"[^28]—this "not departing from this mind" is the true referent of "remaining in the world." What remains is not Śākyamuni Buddha's physical form; what remains is this sealing-function, and this sealing-function has remained, moment by moment, without ever departing.

### ❌ V.3 "Moment-by-Moment Buddhas Attaining Perfect Awakening" ≠ Subjective Idealism

The third danger moves to the opposite extreme: reading "within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening" as "the buddha exists only in my mind; there is no buddha outside"—this is *subjective idealism* in the Western philosophical sense, a position the Huayan school has never held.

In both the *Huayan jin shizi zhang* and the *Wàng jìn huányuán guān*, Fazang repeatedly emphasizes: the "own mind" of which nature-arising speaks is not the "subjective mind" set in opposition to "external objects," but rather the "*dharmadhātu*-mind" that exists *prior* to the formation of any subject-object opposition whatsoever. **The *zì* "own" of "own mind" does not mean "mine privately"; it means "the *dharmadhātu*'s own-self."** "As with one's own mind, so too the minds of all sentient beings"—this very next line in the prose of the "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter is expressly designed to forestall the subjective-idealist reading: the sealing-function acts upon *my* mind, and simultaneously acts upon the minds of *all* sentient beings, because this "mind" is not private at all.

### ❌ V.4 Universal Sealing ≠ Pantheistic Equality

The fourth danger is to read "universally sealing all mental activities" as a kind of pantheistic "all things are buddha, no differentiation whatsoever"—a reading that would immediately erase the meaning of practice, since if every mental activity is already a buddha's, what remains to be cultivated?

The *pǔ* "universal" in *pǔ yìn* does not mean "indifferently pervasive"; it means "*subtly responsive* to each differentiation." The sealing-function seals the *arhat*'s mental activity with an *arhat*-corresponding seal; seals the bodhisattva's mental activity with a bodhisattva-corresponding seal; seals the hell-being's mental activity with a hell-being-corresponding seal. The precision of this correspondence is precisely what the two characters *wēixì* "subtlety" in "subtle inclusion and establishment" signify. **Universal sealing preserves differentiation; it does not erase it.** The meaning of practice lies in this: through the acknowledgment of Vow Seven, adjusting one's own mental activity so that it forms a clearer correspondence with this universally-sealing function—*not* in declaring that one "is already a buddha."

### ❌ V.5 Ocean-Seal Samādhi ≠ the Exclusive Property of a Few Sagely Stages

The fifth danger issues from an unconscious hierarchicalism: the assumption that Ocean-Seal Samādhi is a state only bodhisattvas of the tenth *bhūmi* or buddhas can speak of, unrelated to ordinary practitioners.

As shown in §II.A.1, the Ocean-Seal verse is placed, within the original sūtra, in the faith-stage section of the Xianshou chapter—the concluding segment of Bodhisattva Xianshou's reply to Mañjuśrī concerning the merit of the stage of faith. **Ocean-Seal is not the exclusive property of a few sagely stages; Ocean-Seal is a samādhi a faith-stage bodhisattva can already enter.** The "container, not ladder" framework established in S8-P02 operates again here: each position within the fivefold doctrinal classification is the *dharmadhātu*'s description of itself, not a measuring-stick for practitioners. Any tendency to read Ocean-Seal Samādhi as "a state only those in high stages can speak of" is fundamentally at odds with the Xianshou chapter's original scriptural placement.

*Note to S3 Yogācāra readers:* Yogācāra's "transformation of consciousness into wisdom" (*zhuǎn shí chéng zhì* 轉識成智) requires the cultivational sequence of the ten *bhūmis*; Huayan's "sealing-function of Ocean-Seal" can already be acknowledged at the stage of faith. This is not a claim that Huayan is higher or lower than Yogācāra—it is two distinct entry-points into the same dependent origination, and is precisely the internal vindication of the "container proposition" of S8-P02.

## VI. Practice Correspondence — The Gate of Subtle Inclusion and Establishment

The Gate of Subtle Inclusion and Establishment (*wēixì xiāngróng ānlì mén* 微細相容安立門) is one of the Ten Profound Gates.[^29] *Subtlety* is the precision with which the universal-sealing function acts; *inclusion* is the non-obstruction of all images manifesting together; *establishment* is the stable self-standing of each facet within that mutual inclusion. The three operate simultaneously.

The concrete practice of Vow Seven is bringing these three marks into the present moment of daily cultivation. This section selects three representative scenarios, unfolding the simultaneous operation of the three marks in each.

### VI.1 Scenario One — The First Thought Upon Waking

The first thought upon waking is often scattered—bodily sensation, residue of last night's dreams, the day's plans—all tumbling out at once. The usual antidote is "do not let thought arise" or "clear it out the moment it arises," but both antidotes set thought and awakening in opposition.

The practice of Vow Seven is different: at the very moment the first thought rises, gently acknowledge—"*within this thought, a buddha is attaining perfect awakening*."

- **Subtlety**: not imagining the first thought *to be* a buddha, but acknowledging that the sealing-function is universally sealing it with a precision corresponding to this scattered thought.
- **Inclusion**: the scattered thought and "a buddha attaining perfect awakening" do not obstruct each other in this present moment—they are two faces of the same sealing-function.
- **Establishment**: the scattered thought remains in its position (it has not been eliminated); "a buddha attaining perfect awakening" also remains in its position (it has not been petitioned over from outside). Both stand stably in their places.

This is the present-moment practice of Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World. The buddha does not come from outside, because the buddha has never departed; the scattered thought is not negated, because the scattered thought is itself the object being universally sealed.

### VI.2 Scenario Two — The Moment of Prostration

Prostration is among the most universal daily practices in Chinese Buddhism. The common reading is "I prostrate to the buddha; the buddha is over there"—the one who prostrates here, the one prostrated to there, the act of homage in between.

The reading of Vow Seven inverts this structure:

- **Subtlety**: every subtle motion of the prostrating body (joining palms, bending, bringing the head to the ground) is being universally sealed by the sealing-function; the major and minor marks of the buddha are simultaneously being universally sealed.
- **Inclusion**: the one prostrating and the one prostrated to are not two opposed positions within Ocean-Seal; they are two entry-points of the same sealing-function. "The one who pays homage and the one who receives it—their natures are empty and still" (*néng lǐ suǒ lǐ xìng kōng jì* 能禮所禮性空寂)[^30]—this is exactly the doctrine here.
- **Establishment**: the act of prostration remains the act of prostration; the buddha remains the buddha—"inclusion" does not cancel "establishment." The concrete act of homage preserves its conventional-truth integrity intact.

The concrete action of Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World in this scenario is: before, during, and after the prostration, silently acknowledging within the heart, "*within this single prostration, all buddhas are moment-by-moment attaining perfect awakening*"—and this acknowledgment itself is the request. It is not an additional petition-thought; it is reading the act of prostration *itself* as request.

### VI.3 Scenario Three — Facing an Adverse Person

The third scenario is the hardest—facing someone who is hurting you, misunderstanding you, causing aversion to rise in your heart.

The common antidotes are "cultivate patience," "contemplate them as former parents," "contemplate the emptiness of *dharmas*"—all effective antidotes, but all of them require first pressing down the present aversion.

The practice of Vow Seven does not require first pressing down aversion. At the very moment aversion arises, gently acknowledge: "*this person's mental activity is also within the universal sealing of Ocean-Seal*."

- **Subtlety**: the sealing-function has a corresponding seal for this person's harming mental activity in this moment, and a corresponding seal for your aversion in this moment. Each of the two mental activities is being universally sealed with its own corresponding precision.
- **Inclusion**: harmer and harmed, perpetrator and victim—within Ocean-Seal they are not two opposed poles but two positions at which the same sealing-function is acting.
- **Establishment**: the harm remains harm (the conventional judgment is not cancelled); the aversion remains aversion (your own feeling is not denied). But beneath this "remains" there is now an additional layer—an acknowledgment. This acknowledgment does not change the event, but it changes your relation to the event.

This practice is not telling you to forgive immediately, to let go immediately—these are later fruits. It is telling you to do only this, right now: acknowledge. **This person's mental activity, in this moment, is also being universally sealed by Ocean-Seal; a buddha is attaining perfect awakening here and now, in this very place.** The acknowledgment itself is Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World. The appearance of an adverse person is, doctrinally within Huayan, one concrete gate at which Samantabhadra's Vow Seven can be practiced.

### VI.4 Continuously Studying the Buddha Within the Three Marks

S8-P11 (*The Ten Great Vows under the Ten Profound Gates*) has already treated the mutual inclusion of the ten vows. The relation between Vow Seven and Vow Eight ("Continuously Studying the Buddha," *cháng suí fó xué* 常隨佛學) is especially close: because "continuously studying the buddha" requires first acknowledging "the buddha is moment-by-moment present"—and that acknowledgment is precisely Vow Seven. Without Vow Seven, continuously studying the buddha degrades into "following in the footsteps of some historical buddha"; with Vow Seven, continuously studying the buddha becomes "following the moment-by-moment present sealing-function itself."

This point will be developed further in S8-P08 (*Six Characteristics in Perfect Fusion*) through the structure of the six characteristics—the simultaneous operation of the six characteristics is the structural face of "continuously studying the buddha." This paper does not duplicate that treatment.

## VII. Conclusion

On the rain-drenched night at Cāpāla, Ānanda was silent three times. Three times the Buddha said "I am able to remain a kalpa or more"; three times what he received was silence. After the silence, Māra Pāpīyas requested the Buddha to enter *parinirvāṇa*, and the Buddha consented. When Ānanda returned and again requested, the Buddha said, "what has already been cast off, already discharged," and sternly declared, "the fault is precisely yours." This is "requesting to remain" at the Āgama level—a missed opportunity, a gate that once existed but has passed beyond recovery.

On the Huayan assembly, Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, facing the same question, gave another answer. The "Manifestation of the Tathāgata" chapter says "within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening," and again, "bodhi universally seals all mental activities; therefore it is called perfect awakening." The seventh of Samantabhadra's Ten Great Vows, "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World," is *not* a retrospective attempt to catch up on Ānanda's old forfeit. It is an acknowledgment of a fact that has never been lost and cannot possibly be lost—**that sealing-function is present moment-by-moment, and has never departed**.

Āgama and Huayan do not conflict. The rain-drenched night at Cāpāla did occur; Śākyamuni Buddha did enter *parinirvāṇa* at Kuśinagara; these are unalterable historical facts. Huayan does not negate the factual layer of the Āgama; Huayan only replaces these facts within the temporal structure of Ocean-Seal—the temporal structure of three-time radiant manifestation. Within this structure, "the past Buddha entering *nirvāṇa*" and "the present Buddha remaining in the world" are not sequential but simultaneous. The Buddha's entry into *nirvāṇa* is itself being sealed, continuously, within Ocean-Seal; but this sealing does not obstruct the "buddha attaining perfect awakening" of this present thought-moment from being sealed simultaneously alongside it.

**Vow Seven is not petition but acknowledgment**. Its concrete action is not the addition of another petition-thought, but the reading of every thought-moment, every prostration, every encounter with an adverse being as a concrete gate for "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World." Subtlety, inclusion, establishment—the precision of the sealing-function acting upon every differentiated mental activity; the non-obstruction between differentiation and differentiation; each differentiation standing stably in its own position.

Fazang wrote, in fascicle 4 of the *Huayan wujiao zhang*, "*this rests upon Ocean-Seal Samādhi, whence all is brilliantly and simultaneously manifested*." The true meaning of this sentence waits to be read only when a reader, at some one moment of prostration, some one morning awakening, some one encounter with a person they find repellent, gently acknowledges "*here, a buddha is attaining perfect awakening*."

The next paper, S8-P08 (*Six Characteristics in Perfect Fusion*), will unfold the "simultaneous operation" structural face latent within subtle inclusion and establishment using the tool of the six characteristics (whole, part, identity, difference, formation, destruction). The relation between Vow Seven and the six characteristics is the relation between "the sealing-function" and "the structural face of the sealing-function"—the former is the verb, the latter is the shape of the verb.

> The awesome spiritual power of Ocean-Seal Samādhi—
> Thought after thought, always a buddha attaining perfect awakening;
> The universal sealing of all mental activities is no mirror-image:
> A single prostration, a single thought—each may request.

## Cross-Series Index

- **S3 Yogācāra series:** §III.1 places Chengguan's first meaning "depth of essence, like the ocean" alongside Yogācāra's description of the *ālayavijñāna* as "ever-turning like a torrent"—the torrent being the horizontal continuity of time, Ocean-Seal being the vertical bottomlessness of subject-object. §V.5 further contrasts "transformation of consciousness into wisdom requires the sequential ten *bhūmis*" with "the sealing-function of Ocean-Seal can be acknowledged at the stage of faith," noting that these are two entry-points into the same dependent origination, not a higher/lower ladder.
- **S6 *Śūraṅgama* series:** §IV.4 notes the structural convergence between Ocean-Seal Samādhi and Avalokiteśvara's Perfect Penetration of the Ear-Faculty among the Twenty-Five Perfect Penetrations—both take the subject-capacity (that which manifests, that which hears) itself as the core. "Turning the hearing to hear one's own nature" is the ear-faculty expression of "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World = direct acknowledgment of the sealing-function." The two series converge at doctrinal depth.
- **S8-P01 (*Ocean-Seal Samādhi and the Speaking of the Unspeakable*):** P01 constructs Ocean-Seal as the scriptural-teaching body of the Huayan (Fazang's "Gate of Ocean-Seal Radiant Manifestation" in *Tanxuan ji* fasc. 1). The present paper inherits P01's "sealing-function" thesis and develops it further from the angle of "Vow Seven = present acknowledgment," unfolding the practical face of Ocean-Seal Samādhi in daily cultivation. P01 and P07 together constitute the "essence-and-function double wheel" of Ocean-Seal Samādhi within the S8 series.
- **S8-P02 (*The Yogācāra Position within the Fivefold Classification*):** §V.5 directly invokes the "container, not ladder" framework established in P02 to prevent reading Ocean-Seal Samādhi as "a state only those in sagely stages can speak of." This is also the structural significance of the Xianshou chapter's placement of the Ocean-Seal verse within the faith-stage section.
- **S8-P04 (*Repentance as the Gateway to Nature-Arising*):** §II.A.2 shows that "within one's own mind, thought-moment after thought-moment, there is always a buddha attaining perfect awakening" is both the core scriptural witness of P04's nature-arising doctrine *and* the doctrinal foundation of P07's "Requesting the Buddhas to Remain in the World." Nature-arising and Vow Seven converge within Ocean-Seal Samādhi. P04 enters nature-arising through the action-facet of "concealing" and "disclosing"; P07 enters nature-arising through the view-facet of "acknowledging the sealing-function."
- **S8-P05 (*The Subsumption of Faith-Completion*, forthcoming):** §II.A.1 establishes "Ocean-Seal within the faith-stage section of the Xianshou chapter" as the original scriptural location, prefiguring P05's central argument ("the buddha of practice is not the buddha of stage")—Ocean-Seal is not the exclusive property of a few sagely stages; a faith-stage bodhisattva can already enter this samādhi.
- **S8-P08 (*Six Characteristics in Perfect Fusion*):** §VI.4 notes the relation between Vow Seven and Vow Eight "Continuously Studying the Buddha"—without Vow Seven (acknowledgment that the buddha is moment-by-moment present), continuously studying the buddha degrades into "following in the footsteps of some historical buddha." P08 will further unfold the simultaneous-operation face of "continuously studying the buddha" through the six-characteristics structure, taking up the three practice scenarios of §VI.
- **S8-P11 (*The Ten Great Vows under the Ten Profound Gates / Synoptic Reading of the Ten Profound Gates*):** §III.5 leaves six of Chengguan's ten meanings to be treated together with the overall structure of the Ten Profound Gates in P11. §IV.1's establishment of "Ocean-Seal is not one gate within the Ten Profound Gates; Ocean-Seal is the root of all ten" is a presupposition of P11's synoptic statement.
