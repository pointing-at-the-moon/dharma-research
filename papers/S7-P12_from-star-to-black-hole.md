## Abstract

This paper concludes the twelve-paper arc of S7 (*Lotus Sūtra: The Gathering*) of the Pointing at the Moon project. Unlike the preceding eleven papers, P12 establishes no new doctrinal thesis. It performs two structural tasks: it formally consolidates Proposition Two—that the *Saddharmapuṇḍarīka Sūtra* 妙法蓮華經 and the *Buddhāvataṃsaka Sūtra* 大方廣佛華嚴經 constitute a functional star–black hole duality—and it hands the eighth-arc baton to S8. The argument draws on the Lotus's four "transmission division" (流通分) chapters (*Stūpa* 見寶塔品, *Tathāgata's Supernatural Powers* 如來神力品, *Entrustment* 囑累品, *Encouragements of Samantabhadra* 普賢菩薩勸發品), with the opening *Prologue* chapter (序品) forming a closing inclusion; on the Āgama "one path" (一道) formula in the *Madhyama-āgama Satipaṭṭhāna-sutta* and the Brahmā-entreaty narratives in the *Dīrgha-āgama* and *Ekottara-āgama* as the evolutionary substrate of Proposition Two; and on the *Lokadhātu-vyūha* chapter (世主妙嚴品) of the Eighty *Avataṃsaka* together with Samantabhadra's appearance in fascicle forty of the Forty *Avataṃsaka* (*Bhadracarī-praṇidhāna* 普賢行願品) as the two handoff gateways—venue and person. Patriarchal commentaries by Zhiyi 智顗 (*Fahua xuanyi* 法華玄義 T1716), Kuiji 窺基 (*Fahua xuanzan* 法華玄贊 T1723), Fazang 法藏 (*Huayan wujiao zhang* 華嚴五教章 T1866), and Chengguan 澄觀 (*Huayan jingshu* 華嚴經疏 T1735) provide the doctrinal scaffolding. Section 5 addresses three Middle-Way corrections—gathering ≠ supremacy, duality ≠ dualism, functional-duality reading vs. hierarchical classification—and Section 6 completes the handoff in two movements (venue and person) and seals the series with a failure-point marker. With P12, the S7 arc *Gathering: The Lotus Sūtra* is formally closed.

**Keywords:** *Saddharmapuṇḍarīka Sūtra*; transmission division; *Stūpa* chapter; *Tathāgata's Supernatural Powers*; *Entrustment*; *Encouragements of Samantabhadra*; Samantabhadra; *sāgara-mudrā-samādhi* (Ocean Seal Samādhi); Brahmā-entreaty; *Madhyama-āgama Satipaṭṭhāna-sutta* "one path that purifies beings"; One Buddha-Vehicle; Proposition Two; gathering–manifesting duality; star–black hole duality; Zhiyi's five-flavor classification; Fazang's distinct/shared one-vehicle; Kuiji's one-vehicle/three-vehicle reading; Chengguan's ten-meaning classification; functional-duality reading vs. hierarchical classification; pedagogical hazard #1 (sectarian supremacy); S7 series conclusion; seventh baton of the Eight-Arc Project.

## 1. Introduction: At the End of S7

The S7 series began in early 2026 and traversed eleven papers. P01 ("True Mark of All Dharmas · The Ten Suchnesses") established the foundation by forging the *Upāyakauśalya* chapter's declaration that "only Buddhas with Buddhas can fully exhaust the true mark of all dharmas" together with the ten-suchnesses formula into the doctrinal bedrock of the entire arc. P02 unfolded "opening, showing, awakening, entering" (開示悟入) into a cosmic-scale awakening loop. P03 through P06 worked through the four major parables, establishing the dual-layer §2 methodology of "Āgama seed → Lotus dramatization." P07 ("Robe-Jewel and Topknot-Jewel") set "concealed treasure" and "withheld timing" side by side as two distinct pedagogies of hiddenness. P08 ("Two Wings Displayed Simultaneously"), in handling the Devadatta chapter, upgraded Proposition One from a single Yogācāra three-natures mapping into the "first confluence of the Tathāgatagarbha track and the Yogācāra track in the history of Mahāyāna Buddhism." P09 elevated "universal reverence" from a statement into a practice gate. P10 treated the structure of Avalokiteśvara's responsive manifestation and the etymology of the name. P11 ("Tathāgata Lifespan · The Physician's Sons") capped the basal gate (本門), resolving cleanly the phenomenology of the dharmakāya's appearance relative to time.

By the end of P11, Proposition One had been systematically unfolded (the dual-track confluence, from three-natures mapping to dharmakāya equality), and Proposition Three had been systematically verified (the Āgama seed line being honored point by point in the seven parables and the prediction structures). What remained was Proposition Two—the gathering–manifesting duality—which had been thematized in P01 as a heading, advanced as an undercurrent throughout the subsequent papers, but never formally consolidated. P12's task is to consolidate this line.

P12 is the only exception within S7. It establishes no new doctrine, because S7's doctrinal work is complete; it does not deeply mine any single chapter, because deep mining was the work of P01–P11. It does two things:

First, **it consolidates Proposition Two**. The "Lotus = stellar radiation, Avataṃsaka = black-hole collapse" functional duality, which had been advanced through the entire series as an undercurrent, is brought from the metaphorical layer to the textual-structural layer, and from individual hints to a three-tier evidence chain (intra-textual structure / evolutionary line / patriarchal classification), giving it a formal academic articulation.

Second, **it hands the baton to S8**. The seventh baton of the Eight-Arc Project is passed here. The S8 *Avataṃsaka* series has its venue (the *sāgara-mudrā-samādhi*, Ocean Seal Samādhi) and its person (Samantabhadra) both already in place within the *Avataṃsaka* text itself; P12's task is to point at the door and say "the door is open," then put down the pen.

In its overall posture relative to the Eight-Arc Project, S7 itself is a gathering posture; P12 therefore selects no single mode of failure (no single one of the ten Avataṃsaka gates), but instead allows itself to become "the final demonstration of the gathering posture itself." The ten gates of S8 begin with S8-P01 and do not overlap with S7-P12.

## 2. Textual Foundations

### 2.1 The Lotus Transmission Division: Four Acts of Intra-Textual Consolidation

The "transmission division" (流通分) of the *Lotus*[^2] is variously delimited by Tiantai and later commentators; this paper adopts a narrow framing[^1], focusing on the chapters of Stūpa (見寶塔品 §11), Tathāgata's Supernatural Powers (如來神力品 §21), Entrustment (囑累品 §22), and Encouragements of Samantabhadra (普賢菩薩勸發品 §28), with the Prologue chapter (序品 §1) added as a closing inclusion. These five anchors constitute the five intra-textual acts by which the Lotus consolidates itself.

**2.1.1 Stūpa (§11): The Temporal Structure of Co-Presence.** S7-P11 had already used the longitudinal time face of the stūpa structure (the constant abiding of past Buddhas) in handling the *Lifespan* chapter. P12 takes its lateral structural face: **the simultaneous co-presence of past and present Buddhas.** Prabhūtaratna (Many-Treasures) Tathāgata had entered parinirvāṇa in the immeasurable past, yet his entire body-relic remained intact within the stūpa; Śākyamuni, preaching the Lotus in the present age, witnesses the stūpa rising in mid-air, splitting half its seat, and sharing it with him. This act is itself a structural declaration: within the temporality of the Lotus, a past Buddha is not a "Buddha who has ended" but a "Buddha simultaneously present in another dimension." The Lotus uses this act to scaffold the eternal-realization thesis of the *Lifespan* chapter, and—this is what P12 wishes to emphasize—it also provides, on the Lotus side, the corresponding point to the temporal structure of "simultaneous mutual containment" (同時具足) as articulated in the Avataṃsaka's *Worthy Leader* chapter through the Ocean Seal verses. In other words: the Lotus's stūpa co-presence ≈ the Avataṃsaka's Ocean Seal simultaneity. The former expresses it through narrative, the latter through samādhi; the former is the way of the star (a scene visible to sentient beings), the latter the way of the black hole (the formless structure of the dharmadhātu). The first intra-textual evidence for Proposition Two is established.

**2.1.2 Tathāgata's Supernatural Powers (§21): The Lotus's Self-Positioning.** As the preaching of the Lotus draws to a close, Śākyamuni manifests the broad-long-tongue mark, emits light from between his brows, and personally declares: "To state it concisely, all the dharmas possessed by the Tathāgata, all the sovereign supernatural powers of the Tathāgata, all the secret essential treasury of the Tathāgata, all the most profound matters of the Tathāgata—all are revealed and openly declared in this sūtra." Pointing at the Moon does not read this self-positioning along the "King of Sūtras" hierarchy line (this is the danger that §5's first Middle-Way correction will address), but takes a functional reading: the two characters "openly declared" (顯說) are decisive—the Lotus positions itself as a sūtra **that openly and completely speaks to humans**, not as "the highest sūtra" but as "the sūtra that speaks most completely to humans." The function of a star is precisely to send light stably and receivably in all directions; the Lotus's "open declaration" is exactly this function. Set against this, the Avataṃsaka's *Lokadhātu-vyūha* opens with the posture of "silent and unmoving"—not silence as not-speaking, but silence as not-speaking-to-the-human-conceptual-mind. The two sūtras' self-positionings are precisely symmetrical: the Lotus self-positions as "complete open declaration to humans," the Avataṃsaka as "speaking silently and unmovingly to the dharmadhātu itself." The second intra-textual evidence for Proposition Two is established.

**2.1.3 Entrustment (§22): The Final Act of Gathering.** The *Entrustment* chapter is the gravitational center of the Lotus's transmission division. Śākyamuni rises from his Dharma-seat and, with his right hand, places it upon the heads of innumerable bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, three times stroking and three times entrusting: "For innumerable hundreds of thousands of myriads of *koṭis* of *asaṃkhyeya kalpas* I have cultivated this hard-to-attain Dharma of *anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi*; I now entrust it to you. After my parinirvāṇa, you should single-mindedly receive, hold, recite, explain, and copy it, and practice it as preached." "Stroking the head and entrusting" is the final—and the most physically embodied—act of gathering. S7's one-character motto, "gathering" (攝), here attains its literal embodiment: the Buddha's hand is actually placed on each bodhisattva's head. Gathering is not abstract doctrine but physical-layer contact; not "I hand over the *Lotus Sūtra* as a text," but "I hand over the continuum of this Dharma, person by person, into your hands." A small but important observation arises here: the recipients are "innumerable bodhisattva-mahāsattvas," but the act is **one-by-one**. This "innumerable × one-by-one" structure is itself the miniature of Proposition Two. The defining feature of stellar radiation is exactly this: it shines simultaneously upon innumerable beings, yet what each being receives is "the ray of light sent for that one being alone." The Lotus's gathering structure here attains its highest intra-textual density.

**2.1.4 Encouragements of Samantabhadra (§28): The Handoff Posture in Place.** The *Encouragements of Samantabhadra* is the Lotus's final chapter. Samantabhadra Bodhisattva comes from the eastern Buddha-land of Ratnatejā­bhyudgatarāja Tathāgata, riding a six-tusked white elephant, enters the Sahā world, arrives before Śākyamuni, and asks: "World-Honored One, after the Tathāgata's parinirvāṇa, how can good men and good women obtain this *Lotus Sūtra*?" Śākyamuni preaches the four dharmas of attainment, and Samantabhadra assumes the role of guardian: "I shall guard them, removing their afflictions and granting them ease." He grants prediction to those who hold the Lotus: "Such a person shall not long thereafter proceed to the bodhimaṇḍa, destroy the host of Māras, and attain *anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi*." S7-P09 has already addressed Sadāparibhūta as the exemplar of "universal reverence as practice gate"; what P12 wishes to point out here is something at another layer—**the Lotus ending with Samantabhadra's appearance is not accidental, it is an intra-textual structural choice for handoff**. Samantabhadra is one of the principal figures at the Avataṃsaka assembly (paired with Mañjuśrī as one of Śākyamuni's two flanking bodhisattvas), the final teacher visited by Sudhana in the *Gaṇḍavyūha*'s pilgrimage of fifty-three teachers, and the bodhisattva who proclaims the Ten Great Vows in the *Bhadracarī-praṇidhāna* of the Forty *Avataṃsaka*. The Lotus's last act is Samantabhadra's entrance; the Avataṃsaka's opening and closing both have a place for Samantabhadra. The "entering" act of the same bodhisattva between these two sūtras is in itself the textual-layer handoff posture. §3 will argue for the intra-textual self-evidence of Proposition Two on this basis.

**2.1.5 Prologue (§1): The Closing Inclusion.** The Lotus opens with Mañjuśrī answering Maitreya's question, citing the precedent of the past Buddha Candrasūryapradīpa, who in the immeasurable past had likewise preached the *Lotus*, had likewise manifested these six auspicious signs. Mañjuśrī uses this to encourage faith: as Śākyamuni today manifests these six signs, he too shall preach the *Lotus*. P12 invokes the Prologue as a closing inclusion for this reason: the Prologue says "past Buddhas have already preached, the present Buddha shall now preach," and the *Entrustment* chapter says "I now entrust, future Buddhas shall preach." Reading along this "already preached—shall preach—shall be preached in the future" line, the Lotus itself is a star continuous through the three times—the *Lotus* preached by Candrasūryapradīpa, the *Lotus* preached by Śākyamuni, and the *Lotus* entrusted forward to the future, are the same beam of light radiating from the same source across different temporal dimensions. This is the temporal structure of the Lotus: continuous through the three times with invariant function. The mutual answering of Prologue and *Entrustment* locks the *Lotus* itself into a closed stellar system. P12's pen-laying-down act in §6 will mirror this closed posture.

### 2.2 The Āgama Line of "the One"

**2.2.1 *Madhyama-āgama* Satipaṭṭhāna-sutta: One Path That Purifies Beings.** *Madhyama-āgama* fascicle 24, *Satipaṭṭhāna-sutta*, opens: "There is a one path that purifies beings, that leads beyond sorrow and fear, that extinguishes suffering and affliction, that severs weeping, that attains the right Dharma—namely, the four foundations of mindfulness."[^3] The phrase "one path" is the most ancient Āgama-side seed of the Lotus's "one Buddha-vehicle." S7-P02 has already elaborated this line; this paper does not repeat the full exposition, only invokes it as the seed prototype of the "one" of Proposition Two: the "one Buddha-vehicle" is not a Mahāyāna fabrication, but the dramatic, cosmicized, structural unfolding of the Āgama "one path that purifies beings." From the Āgama's "one path" to the Lotus's "one Buddha-vehicle" is the same "one" at two intensities of disclosure.

**2.2.2 The Brahmā Entreaty: The Most Ancient Foundational Evidence for Proposition Two.** Both the *Mahāparinibbāna-sutta* of the *Dīrgha-āgama* and the *Adhyeṣaṇā-vagga* of the *Ekottarika-āgama*[^4] record a critical episode after Śākyamuni's awakening: under the bodhi tree, having attained complete awakening, the Buddha first contemplates that the Dharma he has realized is too profound and difficult to grasp, that beings are attached to the five desires and hard to bring to faith, and so he initially intends not to turn the wheel of the Dharma, but to enter the silent nirvāṇa; then Brahmā Sahāmpati descends and three times beseeches him to preach, whereupon the Buddha consents and proceeds to Vārāṇasī to preach the Four Noble Truths to the five bhikṣus.

This passage is the true crux of P12. It is not a "seed"—it is directly the Āgama-side internal evidence of Proposition Two.

The *Avataṃsaka*'s *Lokadhātu-vyūha* chapter declares: after the Buddha's awakening, he first preached the *Avataṃsaka* in the Ocean Seal Samādhi, with the audience consisting of great bodhisattvas and devas; humans simply could not hear it, so the Buddha "stepped back" to Mṛgadāva to preach the Āgama. This declaration has long been read in academic circles as "a myth fabricated by the Avataṃsaka school to elevate the *Avataṃsaka*." But the Brahmā-entreaty episode is the Āgama's own record—the Āgama itself acknowledges that the Buddha's first action after awakening was **silence**, was the **initial intention not to preach**.

Reading these two passages together: the Āgama side says "initial intention not to preach"; the Avataṃsaka side says "already preached in the Ocean Seal Samādhi, humans cannot hear it." Both expressions point to the same fact: what occurred immediately after awakening was not Āgama-level preaching, but something the human conceptual mind cannot receive. The Āgama records it as "silence"; the Avataṃsaka records it as "the soundless broadcast of the Ocean Seal Samādhi." Two recordings, on different channels, of the same event.

This is the most ancient foundational evidence for Proposition Two at the textual level. The star–black hole duality is not a metaphor invented by later interpreters—it is the structural agreement between the temporal sequence recorded by the Āgama itself and the content claim made by the Avataṃsaka itself.

### 2.3 The Avataṃsaka's First Opening Gate: The Venue Is in Place

The opening passage of the *Lokadhātu-vyūha* chapter of the Eighty *Avataṃsaka*: "Thus have I heard. At one time, the Buddha was in the *araṇya* Bodhimaṇḍa of Magadha, having just attained complete awakening … entering the *kṣaṇa-pratyutpanna* Buddhas' samādhi, by the spiritual power of all-knowing wisdom manifesting the body of the Tathāgata, pure and unobstructed … the bodhi tree towering and exceptional …" After this passage, the chapter unfolds the assembly of all the host-deities of the dharmadhātu, each offering praise. The posture of the entire opening passage is: the Buddha sits silent and unmoving, the dharmadhātu manifests itself.

S8-P01 ("The Ocean Seal Samādhi and the Speaking of the Unspeakable") has already fully unfolded the doctrinal structure of this venue[^5]. P12 here only invokes its opening posture, as the indication that "the Avataṃsaka's venue is in place"—the Ocean Seal Samādhi is the doctrinal object of S8-P01, not P12's work. P12's syntax here is: "pointing at the door, saying the door is open."

### 2.4 The Avataṃsaka's Second Opening Gate: The Person Is in Place

The opening passage of fascicle 40 of the Forty *Avataṃsaka*, *Bhadracarī-praṇidhāna*: "Then Samantabhadra Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, having extolled the supreme merits of the Tathāgata, addressed the bodhisattvas and Sudhana, saying: 'Good son! The merits of the Tathāgata, even were all the Buddhas of the ten directions, throughout *kalpas* numbering as the dust-particles of unspeakably-many unspeakably-many Buddha-lands, to expound them continuously, could never be exhausted. If one wishes to accomplish this gate of merit, one should cultivate ten kinds of vast practice-vows. What are these ten? First, to revere all Buddhas; second, to praise the Tathāgatas; third, to make extensive offerings; fourth, to repent of karmic obstructions; fifth, to rejoice in merit; sixth, to entreat the turning of the Dharma-wheel; seventh, to entreat the Buddhas to abide in the world; eighth, to constantly follow the Buddhas in study; ninth, to perpetually accord with sentient beings; tenth, to universally transfer all merits.'"

This is Samantabhadra's "act of vow-making" within the *Avataṃsaka*. P12 invokes this passage without unfolding the content of the ten vows (that is the work of S8-P01 through P10), only marking three things:

First, what Samantabhadra does first in the final fascicle of the *Avataṃsaka* is to proclaim the Ten Great Practice-Vows.[^6] This sequence of ten vows is the structural framework for the editorial sequence of S8 papers P01–P10 (S8_PLAN v2.1, Methodological Declaration Four). In other words, Samantabhadra's first action on the Avataṃsaka side is precisely the entire structure of the S8 series.

Second, this Samantabhadra who proclaims the ten vows is the same bodhisattva as the Samantabhadra who, in the Lotus's *Encouragements of Samantabhadra* chapter, comes from the east riding the six-tusked white elephant.

Third, therefore the S7 → S8 handoff is not an academic arrangement fabricated by interpreters; it is a textual fact: Samantabhadra appears at the end of the Lotus to guard the Lotus-holder, and at the end of the Forty *Avataṃsaka* to proclaim the Ten Great Practice-Vows—the same bodhisattva's two consecutive actions, linking the two sūtras. P12's task is only to point this out.

## 3. Core Argument: The Formal Consolidation of Proposition Two

### 3.1 Three Tiers of Evidence

Proposition Two—that the Lotus and the Avataṃsaka constitute the functional duality of "star and black hole"—was advanced as an undercurrent through the first eleven papers of S7. P12 formally consolidates it into three tiers of evidence.

**First tier · Intra-textual structure.** The Lotus's *Tathāgata's Supernatural Powers* chapter's self-positioning declaration ("all are revealed and openly declared in this sūtra") and the Avataṃsaka's *Lokadhātu-vyūha* opening posture ("silent and unmoving") form a functional symmetry. The Lotus's "open declaration" is the function of the star (radiating light stably in all directions, receivable by sentient beings); the Avataṃsaka's "silence" is the function of the black hole (soundless broadcast within the dharmadhātu, unreceivable by the human conceptual mind). This is not the high-low difference between two sūtras, but the explicit division of labor between two pedagogical functions.

**Second tier · Evolutionary line.** From the *Madhyama-āgama*'s "one path that purifies beings" to the Lotus's "one Buddha-vehicle" is the dramatic expansion of "the one"; from the Āgama's "Brahmā entreaty, initial intention not to preach" to the Avataṃsaka's "Ocean Seal Samādhi, silent and unmoving" is the two records of "the speaking of the unspeakable." These two lines share a common Āgama source and unfold separately at the Mahāyāna level into the functional duality of the Lotus and the Avataṃsaka.

**Third tier · Patriarchal classification.** Zhiyi, Kuiji, Fazang, and Chengguan—representing Tiantai, Faxiang/Yogācāra, early Huayan, and late Huayan respectively—although taking different positions, agree on this point: that the Lotus and the Avataṃsaka are the two highest sūtras at different levels or with different functions. §3.4 will positively engage these classifications and articulate the side-by-side relationship between Pointing at the Moon's functional-duality reading and the patriarchal classifications.

### 3.2 Three Pairs of Star–Black Hole Contrasts

| Aspect | Lotus (Star) | Avataṃsaka (Black Hole) |
|---|---|---|
| Structure | Outward radiation | Inward collapse |
| Pedagogical posture | Father speaking to children | Reality speaking to itself |
| Signal type | Emitted in receivable form | Infinite information density, unprocessable by conceptual mind |
| Intra-textual act | Stroking heads, person-by-person entrustment | Silent and unmoving, dharmadhātu self-manifesting |
| Temporal structure | Continuous emission across three times (Prologue–Entrustment inclusion) | One thought-moment containing three times (Ocean Seal Samādhi) |
| Audience metaphor | All sentient beings | The dharmadhātu itself |
| One-character motto | Gathering (攝) | Manifesting (顯) |

These three pairs of contrasts are not two independent objects but two manifestation forms of the same gravitational structure (§5's second Middle-Way correction will address this point).

### 3.3 Samantabhadra's Double Appearance

The strongest intra-textual self-evidence of Proposition Two lies in Samantabhadra's double appearance:

In the Lotus's *Encouragements of Samantabhadra* chapter, Samantabhadra comes from the east riding the six-tusked white elephant, asks the Tathāgata "how can one obtain this *Lotus Sūtra*?", and assumes the role of guardian, granting prediction, and guiding to "shall not long thereafter proceed to the bodhimaṇḍa." Samantabhadra's action here is **escort**—escorting the Lotus-holder from the venue of the Lotus toward the bodhimaṇḍa.

In the Forty *Avataṃsaka*'s *Bhadracarī-praṇidhāna*, having extolled the Tathāgata's merits, Samantabhadra addresses the bodhisattvas and Sudhana: "If one wishes to accomplish this gate of merit, one should cultivate ten kinds of vast practice-vows." Samantabhadra's action here is **disclosure**—to those bodhisattvas and Sudhana who have already arrived at the Avataṃsaka assembly, proclaiming the ten paths to accomplishing the gate of merit.

Reading these two acts together: in the final chapter of the Lotus, Samantabhadra "escorts to the next station"; in the final fascicle of the Avataṃsaka, Samantabhadra "issues the map at the next station." These are not two acts performed by two Samantabhadras; they are two consecutive acts performed by the same Samantabhadra. Between the Lotus's escort and the Avataṃsaka's disclosure, there is nothing for the academic interpreter to insert—the texts themselves complete the handoff.

P12's task is only to point this out. Proposition Two does not need P12 to prove it; Proposition Two is already self-evidenced in Samantabhadra's double appearance. P12 only needs to not block the way.

### 3.4 Functional-Duality Reading vs. Hierarchical Classification

Fazang's *Huayan Wujiao Zhang* fascicle one explicitly states: "This one-vehicle teaching arises depending on the Buddha's emergence from the Ocean Seal Samādhi,"[^9] and distinguishes "distinct-teaching one-vehicle" (別教一乘, the Avataṃsaka) from "shared-teaching one-vehicle" (同教一乘, the Lotus). Fazang's classification is clear: the Avataṃsaka is the distinct-teaching one-vehicle, the Lotus is the shared-teaching one-vehicle, the former higher than the latter.

Pointing at the Moon does not adopt Fazang's hierarchical classification, but neither does it oppose Fazang. The reason is that the two readings address different problems. Fazang addresses the **classification problem**—within the Huayan school's internal pedagogical system, how should the various sūtras be ranked? Within this problem-frame, "distinct-teaching one-vehicle vs. shared-teaching one-vehicle" is an internally consistent and rigorously logical answer. Fazang's answer is valid within the Huayan school's internal pedagogical system. Pointing at the Moon addresses the **functional-duality problem**—within the doctrinal architecture of the Eight-Arc Project, what function does the Lotus and the Avataṃsaka each carry? Within this problem-frame, hierarchical classification is not a valid tool, because the question of "who is higher and who is lower" immediately triggers the Middle-Way pressure that §5 will address ("gathering ≠ supremacy"). What Pointing at the Moon needs is a functional description: who does what, for whom, and how. The "star–black hole duality" is precisely such a functional description.

The relationship between the two readings is side-by-side, not opposed. Fazang's hierarchical reading is valid within Huayan's internal pedagogical system; Pointing at the Moon's functional-duality reading is valid within the Eight-Arc Project's doctrinal architecture. S8-P02 ("The Three-Layer Container of Inception, Completion, and Roundness") has fully treated this side-by-side relationship; P12 does not repeat it, only marks here Pointing at the Moon's choice of posture.

On the Tiantai side, Zhiyi in the *Fahua Xuanyi* uses the five-flavors classification to position the Lotus as the "ghee" (醍醐) flavor, and explicitly states: "In that Buddha's lifetime of teaching, the *Avataṃsaka* was preached first and the *Lotus* preached last … in all cases the Lotus is the final teaching, the final flavor."[^7] This formulation is critical for §5's first Middle-Way correction: Zhiyi himself has already shifted the criterion from "high vs. low" to "ripening." "Last preached, last flavor" is not "the highest flavor" but "the flavor at the final ripening stage in the sequence of five flavors." The Lotus is the final stage of the ripened cream's transformation, not "higher than the Avataṃsaka," but "at the final position in the sequence of five flavors." Pointing at the Moon takes over this Tiantai usage: the "gathering" of the Lotus is the gathering of the ripening stage, not the gathering of hierarchical superiority.

Kuiji's *Fahua Xuanzan* fascicle one[^8] reads the one-vehicle and three-vehicles along the Yogācāra track: the three vehicles are pedagogical constructions according to the capacities of beings (parikalpita layer); the one vehicle is the ultimate of dharmakāya equality (pariniṣpanna layer). S7-P08 has already elevated this line to one of the two tracks of Proposition One; P12 does not repeat it, only points out that Kuiji's reading and Pointing at the Moon's functional-duality reading are methodologically cognate—both decompose the Lotus's core act from "hierarchical difference" into "functional division of labor."

Chengguan's *Huayan Jingshu* uses ten meanings to classify the *Avataṃsaka* in relation to other sūtras;[^10] this paper takes only the methodological posture: Chengguan's ten meanings are a positioning description from the Huayan side toward other sūtras, and this positioning description is itself a "functional description" rather than a "hierarchical verdict." The methodological posture that Pointing at the Moon's functional-duality reading inherits is precisely this.

Four patriarchs, four positions, four systems—but at the methodological level they share one thing in common: the valid form of classification is not ranking but functional description. Pointing at the Moon's Proposition Two is a further concretization of this methodological consensus within the Eight-Arc architecture.

## 4. Cross-Disciplinary Bridge: Stellar Radiation and Black-Hole Collapse

⚠️ **This section is a cross-disciplinary aid to reading, not a doctrinal proof. The correspondence between the physics duality and the Buddhist doctrine is structural analogy only and cannot be reduced to identity of objects.**

In modern astrophysics, stars and black holes are two manifestation forms of the same gravitational structure at different mass stages[^11]. A star radiates the energy of core fusion outward in the form of photons, signals "receivable by sentient beings" (detectors, eyes, plants). Within the event horizon of a black hole, matter and information exist in a way that human observational means cannot directly receive; although Hawking radiation is theoretically present, its signal-to-noise ratio against the cosmic background makes direct reception nearly impossible.

Mapping this physics duality onto the Lotus–Avataṃsaka duality: Lotus ≈ star—stably emitting the dharmakāya's "light" (in receivable forms: parables, predictions, dramatic structures). The function of the star is "reachable," not "farthest" or "strongest." Avataṃsaka ≈ black hole—the entire information density of the dharmadhātu is there, but reception requires special conditions (Buddhahood, Ocean Seal Samādhi, frequency-locked with the dharmadhātu). The black hole is not "hidden"; its information density exceeds the reception threshold.

⚠️ The non-reducibility of this analogy must be immediately declared: the physical star and the black hole are two different physical objects with different mass ranges; the Lotus and the Avataṃsaka are not two different objects but two manifestations of the same dharmakāya at two pedagogical functions. The physics duality does not prove the Buddhist doctrine; it only provides an analogical structure familiar to modern readers as a reading aid. The physical star does not "gather" sentient beings, nor does the black hole "openly declare" the dharmadhātu—these are anthropomorphizing reading-aid language, not literal correspondence.

This ⚠️ marker is important, because Proposition Two may be further borrowed by readers after P12, and this section sets clear boundaries for that borrowing.

## 5. Middle-Way Corrections

### ❌ Correction One: Gathering ≠ Supremacy

The most likely slippage of S7's one-character motto "gathering" is being read as a "King of Sūtras" supremacy claim: the Lotus "gathers" all sūtras, therefore the Lotus is supreme over all sūtras.

The remedy need not be sought externally—Zhiyi within the *Fahua Xuanyi* has already blocked this reading from inside Tiantai itself. Zhiyi uses the five flavors (milk, cream, fresh butter, ripened butter, ghee) to classify teachings, and positions the Lotus as the "ghee" flavor. But Zhiyi's explicit formulation is: "In that Buddha's lifetime of teaching, the *Avataṃsaka* was preached first and the *Lotus* preached last … in all cases the Lotus is the final teaching, the final flavor." The syntactic structure of "last preached, last flavor" is temporal, not evaluative. The five flavors are five stages of the milk's transformation; ghee is the final stage, but "final" does not equal "highest"—the five flavors are five necessary stages, and ghee cannot exist without the nutritional base of milk.

Bringing this Tiantai usage to S7's "gathering": the Lotus's gathering is the gathering of the ripening stage. It can gather because the prior stages (including what the Avataṃsaka broadcasts simultaneously on another channel, the Āgama seeds, the Prajñā-deconstruction, the Yogācāra fine-grained analysis) have all completed their respective work. Gathering is not supremacy; it is ripening.

Further: S7's Proposition Two states that the Lotus and the Avataṃsaka are the star–black hole duality. Within this duality, there is no superior–inferior relationship between star and black hole, only functional division of labor. The star lacks the density of the black hole; the black hole lacks the receivability of the star—each occupies an irreplaceable position. The Lotus gathers all sentient beings, because it is the star; the Avataṃsaka manifests the dharmadhātu itself, because it is the black hole. They cannot replace each other, and they cannot defeat each other.

### ❌ Correction Two: Duality ≠ Dualism

The second danger is reading the "gathering–manifesting duality" as a Buddhist version of dualism: two independent supreme objects, mutually opposed or mutually complementary.

The Lotus and the Avataṃsaka are not two objects. They are two manifestation forms of the same dharmakāya at two pedagogical functions. At the fundamental level, "the Śākyamuni who can preach the *Lotus*" and "the Vairocana silent and unmoving in the Ocean Seal Samādhi" are the same Buddha. S7-P11 has already addressed the appearance of the dharmakāya relative to time; the Huayan school takes Vairocana as the dharmakāya-Buddha and Śākyamuni as the response-body, but the two are not "two Buddhas"—they are two manifestations of the same dharmakāya at two manifestation levels.

The physics duality offers a useful analogy at this point (⚠️ still only as reading aid): stars and black holes are not two independent physical objects, but two states of the same gravitational structure under different mass conditions. A star of sufficiently large mass, at the end of its life, collapses into a black hole; they are two chapters of "the same gravitational story." The Lotus and the Avataṃsaka are two pedagogical manifestations of "the same dharmakāya story"—when speaking to sentient beings, manifesting as the Lotus (star); when speaking to the dharmadhātu, manifesting as the Avataṃsaka (black hole).

The duality is functional, not ontological.

### ❌ Correction Three: Functional-Duality Reading vs. Hierarchical Classification

The third danger comes from Pointing at the Moon's own choice—the choice of functional-duality reading over hierarchical classification may be read as "Pointing at the Moon opposes Huayan classification" or "Pointing at the Moon negates Tiantai's five periods and eight teachings."

Neither is the case. §3.4 has elaborated this in detail: Fazang's distinct/shared-teaching one-vehicle classification is valid within the Huayan school's internal pedagogical system; Zhiyi's five-flavors classification is valid within Tiantai's internal pedagogical system; Pointing at the Moon's functional-duality reading is valid within the Eight-Arc Project's doctrinal architecture. The three systems address three different problems and do not negate each other.

> Each paper in this series will, at some point, stop. Where it stops is not negligence; it is reverence. A three-dimensional pen cannot write a ten-dimensional object. What it can do is mark clearly the position where the pen is laid down—so that readers know that, beyond this line, they must rely on their own Dharma-eye.

P12's failure-point marker is placed not in §5 but after the two acts of handoff in §6. The reason is this: P12's failure point is not that some doctrinal object exceeds language, but that "the truly complete understanding of the gathering–manifesting duality must wait until readers themselves have completed S8." P12 cannot complete this understanding for the reader; it can only make the handoff cleanly and then put down the pen.

## 6. Conclusion: Two Acts of Handoff and the Closing of the Pen

### 6.1 First Handoff—The Venue Is in Place

S7's last act is to hand over the venue to S8.

The Lotus's venue is the Sahā world—the world visible to sentient beings. From the Vulture Peak assembly opening the Prologue, to the Many-Treasures Stūpa rising in mid-air in the *Stūpa* chapter, to the temporal depth of eternal-realization in the *Lifespan* chapter, to the person-by-person entrustment of stroking heads in the *Entrustment* chapter—the entire venue of the Lotus is constructed within "the spacetime perceivable by sentient beings." The light of the star must have a dimension along which it can be seen.

The Avataṃsaka's venue is the dharmadhātu itself—a dimension that the human conceptual mind cannot directly receive. The opening of the *Lokadhātu-vyūha* chapter: "The Buddha was in the *araṇya* Bodhimaṇḍa of Magadha, having just attained complete awakening … entering the *kṣaṇa-pratyutpanna* Buddhas' samādhi"—the Buddha sits silent and unmoving, the dharmadhātu manifests itself. S8-P01 has already unfolded the doctrinal structure of this venue. P12 here only points out: that venue is already in place. At the moment the Lotus ends, the Avataṃsaka's Ocean Seal Samādhi does not "begin"—it has never been interrupted; the reader has only now arrived at the position where it can be heard.

To proceed from the Lotus's ending to the Avataṃsaka's opening is not "leaving one room and entering another"; it is "in the same room, switching to another mode of listening." The venue is in place.

### 6.2 Second Handoff—The Person Is in Place

S7's penultimate act is Samantabhadra Bodhisattva's appearance in the Lotus's final chapter.

In the *Encouragements of Samantabhadra*, Samantabhadra comes from the eastern Buddha-land of Ratnatejā­bhyudgatarāja Tathāgata, riding the six-tusked white elephant, assuming the responsibility of guarding the Lotus-holder and guiding such a person to "shall not long thereafter proceed to the bodhimaṇḍa." The Lotus ends with this act—not as a contingent choice, but as an intra-textual structural choice. Samantabhadra is the bodhisattva about to take up the next stage of teaching.

In the *Bhadracarī-praṇidhāna* of the Forty *Avataṃsaka*, having extolled the Tathāgata's merits, Samantabhadra proclaims to Sudhana and the assembly the Ten Great Practice-Vows. These ten vows—revere all Buddhas, praise the Tathāgatas, make extensive offerings, repent karmic obstructions, rejoice in merit, entreat the turning of the Dharma-wheel, entreat the Buddhas to abide in the world, constantly follow the Buddhas in study, perpetually accord with sentient beings, universally transfer all merits—are precisely the editorial framework of S8 papers P01–P10.

The Lotus ends with Samantabhadra entering; the Avataṃsaka unfolds with Samantabhadra vowing. The same bodhisattva's two consecutive acts—this is a textual fact, not an academic arrangement fabricated by interpreters. Pointing at the Moon's only response to this fact is one sentence: point it out. The person is in place.

### 6.3 Failure Point and Pen-Laying-Down

> Each paper in this series will, at some point, stop. Where it stops is not negligence; it is reverence. A three-dimensional pen cannot write a ten-dimensional object. What it can do is mark clearly the position where the pen is laid down—so that readers know that, beyond this line, they must rely on their own Dharma-eye.

P12's pen-laying-down position is here: the venue has been handed over, the person has been handed over, Proposition Two has been consolidated, and the entrance to S8 has been opened. What P12 can do ends here. The truly complete understanding of the gathering–manifesting duality must wait until readers themselves have completed S8—and that understanding is not in P12's pen, nor in the pen of any single S8 paper, but in the readers' own Dharma-eye.

S7's one character is "gathering." The deepest meaning of gathering is not "I take everything in," but "I hand everything out." The Lotus ends with stroking heads and entrusting, ends with Samantabhadra's appearance as its very last act—both of these are acts of "handing out." S7 ends with P12 doing the same act: handing out, in the posture of "to S8, to the reader," all the doctrine, propositions, and structure accumulated across twelve papers.

The S7 series formally concludes here. Twelve papers are written; the three principal lines—Proposition One (the confluence of the Tathāgatagarbha track and the Yogācāra track), Proposition Two (the gathering–manifesting duality), and Proposition Three (the dramatic completion of the Āgama seeds)—have each completed their respective work. The seventh arc of the Pointing at the Moon project, *Gathering: The Lotus Sūtra*, is closed in April 2026.

The venue and the person of S8, *Manifesting: The Buddhāvataṃsaka Sūtra*, are already in place ahead. The road that remains is in S8.

The true mark of all dharmas is just so, originally so.[^12]
